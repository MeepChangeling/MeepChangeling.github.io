Who Is Bar

Barbra Walters is a Terlix mercenary who was raised by a single human father in the Sol system. Her biological parents and their pack were killed by pirates when she was just barely able to walk. Her father was one of the rescue crew and simply couldn't bare to send a baby a hundred light years to be raised by the state. Given her species is highly communal with nerobiological effects based on their own species presence, as well as a mentality quite different from humans in a number of key areas, Bar (as her friends call her) is something of a mess.

Barbra Walters communicates the way a Terlix communicates. She says exactly what she means and nothing else. Humans see this as a dry wit, or emotional coldness. It's neither. Terlix communicate bluntly with words, but subtly with actions. Bar has never told a joke on purpose in her life, but she's made countless jokes. Most of which went unobserved.

Terlix facial expressions don't map cleanly onto what humans expect to see, and Bar gave up trying to bridge that gap somewhere in her childhood. If Bar is glad to see you, she shows up when you call her. If Bar is worried about you, she's there before you asked. These subtle acts mix with her very obvious expressions of her current state of mood. You can tell when she's happy, scared, aroused, but almost never why. It's very common for her to be labeled a sociopath. Or autistic. People who understand how to interact with autistic people get along with Bar just fine. Those who can't find her unsettling. Bar does not consider this her problem to solve.

Bar spent the first eighteen years of her life as the only member of her species most people she knew had ever met. Needless to say, the Merica colony had no idea what to do with a Terlix kitten. This wasn't for a lack of trying. Her father spent years reasurching what to do and trying different methods. While English and Uli are easily converted to one another, the cultural nuances of Terran and Terlix are not. Many understandable mistakes were made by a man trying his best. This left Bar with a restlessness that never fully resolved, even after the trip to Gulat that taught her what normal looked like for her own people. She found her own kind, but not her "place". Terlix friendship is rare and instinctive. It depends on both individual personalities, and a semi-random factor invovling how pharamones are decoded by the olfactory center. It cannot be rushed or manufactured.

Bar spent decades being a Terlix without ever having the thing her biology is built around. This warped the triggers her brain searches for. Her upbringing imparted behaviors her species often lacks. She should have made a couple friends during her stay on her homeworld. But it didn't happen, so she moved on, turning to mercenary work to try and find something she could feel as intimately about as the average Terlix feels about their friends. Which is why her efforts were directed towards taking down the pirate fleet that made her an orphan in the first place.

Her actions taken against Nova Wing were, in retrospect, more important and helpful for others than herself. She didn't find her place among the derbies of their ships. No. That was at the side of a rebel with a cause in a remote system in the ass end of space. Captain Kit, her first friend, and, so far, her only one. Bar does not consider this complicated or worth dwelling on at length. She found her person. She may never find another, and so long as she has Kit, that's okay.

Bar still hasn't fully resolved the tension between how she was raised and what she is. Terran lessons and Terlix instincts pull in different directions often enough that Bar has simply learned to notice which one is talking and go with whichever makes more sense in the moment. Usualy it's whatever would help or please Kit the most. While this would be a red flag for a human, that's just how Terlix are.

Appearance

Bar would very easily lost in a crowd of her own people. Brown skin, white hair, blue eyes, average height, weight, and build. The most noteworthy thing about her is her left arm. Her left arm is a prosthetic, acquired under circumstances she describes (under the advisement of her attorney) as a "tragic table saw accident" that occurred, by complete coincidence, two days after she discovered a store that sold personal arm lasers as a mod for cybernetic arms. Many people think this is a joke. The truth is Bar saw a functional arm-mounted laser, understood in one sudden and total moment that mechanical augmentation could outperform flesh in ways she actually needed, and decided the upgrade was not optional. This is, by her own account, the only piece of cyberware she has ever wanted or sought out. She is not a collector of augmentations. She wanted exactly one thing, got it, and has had no further interest since.

Despite this, she chose an obviously mechanical limb, one with some synthskin, but obvious joints, and a very obviously mechanical hatch for her new weapon to emerge from. At a glance, one might not notice her limb, mistaking it for a bracer and a glove. But a close look shows it for exactly what it is. Bar likes the dualism, and sees it as a metaphor for herself. She is also, against her usual restraint, slightly embarrassed by how much she loves her laser. It is the one subject that reliably gets more enthusiasm out of her than anything else. A few of her family members joke about it being because she's a cat, and thus, likes to chase lasers. Bar refuses do anything to correct this opinion.

The laser's compartment cover occasionally pops open for no discernible reason. Bar has stopped investigating this, though she does find it annoying when she's using her arm as a pocket.

Bar's right arm is functionally also cybernetic. She wears a heavily worn power armor gauntlet of unknown origin and function. She found it while playing as a child and felt a consulship to take it. So she did. She has never removed it save for bathing since the day it first fit. She does not know what it does, where it came from, or whether it does anything at all other than offer protection to an arm. Knowing was never the point. Having it was.

Like most Terlix, she strongly prefers moving on all fours for any distance worth covering. Despite being raised by humans, the instinct to run on all fours and largely move like a quadruped is something she cannot deny. She's often found on all fours, and her hind legs range of movement is very important to her. Consequently she dresses in a breech cloth and biker's chaps, as they leave her hips entirely unrestricted while offering good protection to her legs. She despises "impractical" clothing, especially if it's especially feminine in appearance. She has been known to act like a house cat getting a bath if made to wear a dress.

This is extremely normal for a Terlix female, not that Bar knows that. Their gender psychology is an inversion of the human norm, with females being more dominant and simple while males are more submissive and prefomrantive.

She looks markedly different when freshly woken from a nap, an observation made independently by everyone who has ever woken her from one. She has, on at least one documented occasion, chased a laser pointer. She maintains this was a tactical reflex test and stands by that explanation under all circumstances. Including when being shown video of the incident that conclusively proves there was no time to think before she pounced. This is also consistent with her species psychology.

Relationships

Kit

Kit is Bar's first, and so far the only, friendship of the kind Terlix biology is built around. Bar found her after decades of being a Terlix without the thing her species generally considers fundamental to a functional life, and she does not treat this lightly even though her manner of discussing it remains entirely flat. Kit's presence measurably sharpens Bar's physical and cognitive performance, in the literal, documented way Terlix friendship does for every member of the species lucky enough to experience it. Bar would, and has, done anything Kit needed done. She does not consider this remarkable. She considers it the correct response to having found her person. As do the rest of her species. Though it does hurt when Kit fails to reciprocate this, as the intensity of friendship Terlix express is simply not something most other species can consistently maintain.

K.A.T.

Bar and K.A.T. are genuinely close in their own right, independent of either of their relationships with Kit. The friendship works because neither of them performs social softening for the other's comfort. K.A.T. says exactly what she's thinking with cheerful directness, and Bar says exactly what she's thinking with flat literalism. Their normal modes of existence thus mesh. Conversations between them tend to move quickly and skip several steps most people would consider necessary. Both find this restful. K.A.T. has stated, on the record, that she finds Bar's ship cute. Bar has not commented on this, but has changed her ship's paint job to better align with K.A.T.'s sense of aesthetics.

Her Father

Bar's relationship with Clint (the man who raised her) remains loving and genuinely complicated. He did everything he could for a Terlix kitten with no other reference point and no community to draw on. The gap between what he could offer and what she actually needed was never his fault, exactly, but it was real and it shaped her regardless. They still speak. He is proud of her, takes joy in the fact she's finally found her footing. Even if the footing in question looked nothing like what he expected. Bar appreciates everything he did for her, and his continued support of her endeavors (which, as a citizen of a post-scarcity society is absolutely nothing to sneeze at).

The Council and Terlix Society

Bar respects the Council of Elders the way she respects any structure that's earned its authority by deed. However, she doesn't feel as if it holds authority over her. The Council are elders but not her elders in any meaningful sense. She is Terlix by every biological measure and by her own clear sense of identity, but she did not grow up inside the culture the Council governs, and has never felt the need to pretend otherwise. To many governments, this would be insulting or shameful. To the Council, they'd be worried for Bar if she felt any other way.

Beliefs

Spirituality

Bar holds the standard Terlix position on matters of belief. A statement of fact deserves proof. A statement without proof is, to her, functionally a lie, and lies are insults whether or not the person making them intends offense. This makes Bar a poor target for most religions, as she is instinctively driven by empirical evidence. Despite her inclinations she remains respectful of others beliefs, until they ask her for her opinions. In which case she comes across as dismissive, though interested in an academic sense about the psychology behind others beliefs.

She finds the Breath of Choll's machine soul debate interesting as a case study in how seriously other species will argue about things neither side can demonstrate. She has never said as much to Kit for the sake of their friendship.

Work

Bar no longer does mercenary work to find something to feel intensely about. Instead, she does it because she's good at it. Effectiveness is its own justification. She doesn't romanticize it the way some mercenaries do, and she doesn't apologize for it the way others feel obligated to. Predators who cry over having killed are not something evolution can abide, and Terlix are, after all, predators. It's a function she performs well, in service of people and causes she's chosen deliberately. She has not found a reason to examine them further.

Loyalty

If Bar has a guiding philosophy beyond pure function, it's the power of friendship. Friendship is real, friendship is everything, and everything else is downstream of it. She does not need a belief system beyond that. She has Kit. She has K.A.T., in her own separate way. She has work that uses her well. The rest is superfishal details.

Current Status

Bar is currently active in the field alongside Kit and K.A.T., operating under the same arrangement with Sam of Sol. The work suits her. She has not commented on whether she considers herself formally part of Sam's assessment of Kit, but she has noted, accurately, that the assignments have so far required nothing she hasn't already proven she can do.

Like Kit and K.A.T., she maintains her home base on the liberated southern continent of Theril III between assignments. She finds the arrangement satisfactory, but has mailed various infrastructure improvement plans to those who could implement them. Including a 842 page long manifesto of sorts which boils down to "please stop doing trains badly."

History

Bar was orphaned before she was old enough to walk reliably. Her birth pack's transport was raided by Nova Wing, a pirate organization typically written off as cartoonishly evil monsters by those who don't care to examine the people currently murdering them for profit. Nova Wing does not operate on ideology, grudges, or cruelty for its own sake. They operate on profit, full stop, with a genuine and apparently sincere sense of family loyalty among their own that simply does not extend one atom past their ranks. Everyone outside Nova Wing is inventory. Bar's pack were simply in the way of profits, so they were killed, which is something that still pisses her off to this day.

One of the rescue workers who responded to the distress call was a human named Clint. He could not bring himself to send an infant Terlix a hundred light years to be raised by the state, so he didn't. He adopted her instead, and raised her largely alone in the Merica colony on the Sol Dyson Sphere. Bar was the only Terlix anyone there had ever personally met. There was no Terlix community to draw on, no mentor to consult, and only whatever fragments of Protectorate documentation existed on a species that, while known, was not common in human space in those days. Clint did the only thing available to him: he researched everything he could find and improvised the rest, one mistake and correction at a time.

Terlix and Terran cultural norms do not translate cleanly even when the languages do, and a single dedicated father working from documentation rather than lived experience could only get so close. What came out of this difficulty, eventually and without either of them seeking it, was useful to others. Central, the Protectorate's governing AI, noticed the pattern across Clint's case and others like it over time, and the gaps in support for isolated Terlix adoptees were quietly closed as a result. Future Terlix kittens raised outside their species' care would have resources Bar never did.

At eighteen, Clint mistakenly believed his daughter had reached adulthood. A reasonable assumption by human standards, but extremely incorrect by Terlix standards. Wanting her to understand her birth culture properly rather than through holograms and reports (the only resources available for someone who didn't want a nanite hive implanted, and thus, cannot download engrams directly), he gave her a ship and sent her to Gulat.

She spent several years there. She learned that everything about her that the Merica colony had found odd was, among her own people, completely normal. The literalism, the quadrupedal preference, the difficulty with performed expression. What she did not find, despite being surrounded by her own species for the first time in her life, was a bond. Terlix friendship depends on a factor that can't be forced or guaranteed, and sometimes it simply doesn't happen.

Having confirmed that nothing was wrong with her, Bar decided to do something with the confirmation. She turned her attention to Nova Wing and spent years building a career as a mercenary, developing the skills and reputation that would eventually let her operate against targets well above what most independent operators could handle.

She eventually took down a Nova Wing capital ship. She didn't hunt it. It was an unexpected complication to an operation unrelated to Nova Wing. Bar was able to board the ship, set charges on a secodary reactor, and made it out, the ship blowing up behind her as the pirates failed to disarm her explosives. It settled something in her, professionally and personally. She considered retiring. while debating moving on to another career, she heard about a Cholli rebellion gathering in a remote system on the far edge of known space, decided it sounded like a good time, and went to help.

She found Kit there and for the first time in forty-three years, the bond her entire biology had been looking for manifested. In Bar's opinion, her story is done. Now it's their story until fate chooses how it ends.
