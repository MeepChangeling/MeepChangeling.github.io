Who Is Kit

Kit of Theril III is the most famous (also infamous) Cholli in the galaxy. Her government classifies her as a terrorist and has authorized its agents to kill her on sight. Her people have deemed her a folk hero, especially on her home world. The Galaxy at large knows her as the latest agent employed by the legendary Terran Immortal Sam, the right hand of Central herself. Her friends know her as the best damn Team Mom in the Orion Arm.

If you asked her about herself, she's say she's a person who decided to do the right thing. Kit is quite humble about her accomplishments. She wont tell you they're not big deals, but she will say she thinks anyone else who put in the work she did could achieve them. This includes her greatest work, liberating a large section of Theril III from her home world's government, thus creating the first free nation of her people in 800 years.

In many people, this mindset would be the result of an internal trauma, or an inability to internalize one's own accomplishments. Much of the galaxy believes Kit suffers from imposer syndrome. She does not. The truth of the matter is Kit counts decades of her early life as perpetration for what she did, and thus, work others would have to put in too. To Kit, her work isn't just the series of battles, endless blockade runs, and formation of resistance groups. It's also her entire childhood playing hero, the decade she spent working as a house servant and everything it taught her, and in essence, her entire life. Including her accident.

Kit is a cyborg. A very obvious one, and not for personal ascetic reasons. In the early days of her youth, Kit worked as a servant for a minor political figure. One day she was ordered to move a new mattress into her boss's bedroom and set up the bed. Following instructions to the letter as always, Kit went to remove the mattress's tag, could not find a cutting tool, and so chose to rip it off. The sharp tug within the geofence of her boss's bedroom caused a bomb hidden in the mattress to detonate.

Kit survived at the cost of her arm, her eye, and half her skin. While in the hospital she learned that her boss had requested she be given a rejuvenating treatment that would have restored her to prefect health, a regrown arm and eye included, but wasn't allowed too. Learning that even the elites of her society had strict rules they were bound by, such that they couldn't even reward someone they saw as saving their lives as they would like too, is the root of everything Kit did thereafter. The moment she decided that if both hard work and the luck of being born in the right demographic wasn't enough, that her society had to change. No matter the cost.

This could be the origin story for any radical or terrorist in history. What made Kit a successful revolutionary is her nature as a person. She grew up reading comics about teams of heroes who won not merely by skill at arms, but through their teamwork and friendship. As a result, she curated like minded friends, not fellow radicals.

Kit made a deliberate choice about what kind of person she wants to be and applies it consistently. She collects people. Crew, allies, contacts, strays. But only if they too seek comrades first and foremost. Her orbit tends to accumulate individuals who needed someone to give a damn about them specifically, and she obliges. Her inner circle is small and fiercely held. Her extended network is labyrinthine and seemingly endless, but equally genuine.

In essence, Kit "knows a guy" for seemingly anything and everything, and that guy will think of her less as a customer, and more of a genuine friend. Albeit a busy one who can't come around that often.

K.A.T. is, depending on who you ask, a ship, a person, a cybernetic organism, a synthetic life form, a tentacle monster playing hermit crab with a starship, or Kit's wife. She is an experimental prototype cybernetic starship commissioned by the Gelsoft Corporation and never intended to be anything other than an experiment in possible next-gen navigation and operations systems. She began as organic tissue threaded through a ship's superstructure, growing in tandem with the vessel's mechanical systems as both developed. The fact that she turned out to also be a sapient being that identified itself as a person was, from Gelsoft's perspective, a complication. From Kit's perspective, it was the second best thing that ever happened to her (Narrowly beaten out by finding a copy of Awesome Comics no. 1 in mint condition, which she was able to sell and got her family a house rather than an apartment.).

Note: While K.A.T.'s name is an acronym, she wasn't told nor programmed with the acronym itself, only that K.A.T. stood for something. As a result she has no idea what her name is aside from K.A.T., and as her creators did not survive the War of Liberation, that knowledge has been lost to time. Fortunately, she doesn't care.

When Kit boarded the ship with the intention of stealing it, K.A.T. was frightened. She did not know what this stranger would do upon realizing the ship was alive. Kit, for her part, did not know what to do with a ship that self-identified as a woman. Other than to start asking her nicly to do normal ship things. The early months were rocky in the specific way that two people who have fundamentally surprised each other by existing tend to be rocky. As Kit needed a ship, and K.A.T. wanted freedom and respect, and both were willing to provide the other with what they wanted, they worked through it. With time they discovered they were rather fond of one another. The rest, as Kit has noted on several occasions, is history, because even in space, even if they are aliens, lesbians still quite often bring a moving truck to their second date.

After Kit made contact with the Terran Protectorate, K.A.T. received substantial upgrades. The Protectorate's position on sapient beings confined entirely within a fixed physical structure is unambiguous, and well known. They consider it cruelty. No different than severing someone's arms and legs, cauterizing the stumps, and throwing them into the middle of a room. As such K.A.T. was provided with a drone replicator system to create as many simple remote bodies as she wishes, and a more sophisticated android body for situations that call for a more conventionally person-shaped presence (like date nights).

K.A.T.'s personality is difficult to summarize without making her sound implausible. She is effortlessly delightful, a fun-loving presence with a warmth so unaffected that even individuals who hold firm beliefs about synthetic lifeforms not being real people tend to find themselves feeling uncomfortable about those beliefs. The most common workaround is to assume she must be remotely operated by someone biological. She finds this funny rather than offensive, which tends to makes it worse for the bigots.

Note: She has no particular interest in cultural identity or belonging. She was built by the Cholli, upgraded by the Protectorate, is married to Kit, and considers none of these things to define her in any categorical sense. She is K.A.T., which she finds entirely sufficient. Several philosophers deeply appreciate her contentment with merely the first first principle of philosophy: "Cogito, ergo sum."

K.A.T. thinks, therefore she is. To her, that is enough.

History

Kit grew up in Koris one of Theril III's major cities, a sprawling urban jungle in which most everyone was functionally anonymous. This suited her fine. She was not remarkable in any way that her society would have recognized. Kit was the definitions of an ordinary child and average citizen. Normal nuclear family. Typical grades. Only notable hobby being her fastidious collection and preservation of comic books.

She chose domestic service as a career the way a practical person chooses any career; she was good at it, it paid well if you worked for someone in high society, and her father (an electrician) had the connections necessary to get her an entry level job in the field. Kit's first job was as a live-in household servant for a minor political figure. She was a hard worker, did her job well enough, and expected to simply work there until she died. Then she was caught up in a Mafia Family's attempt to assassinate her boss.

Kit's employer saw her accidental detonation of the hidden explosive as a "successful protection of her employer". Unlike many of his social class, who would have let Kit simply die or be forced into disabled retirement, arranged for her medical care. Kit learned from her hospital bed that her employer had attempted to provide her with rejuvenation treatment (a full restoration, regrown limbs included) and had been legally prohibited from doing so. The technology was not permitted to be used on anyone of a lower social class, not merely by someone of a lower social class.

This is the moment Kit has described as the root of everything that followed. Not the bomb. Not the pain. The realization that even the people with power were shackled by a system that decided, categorically, that someone like her didn't deserve to be more than a source of labor. If the rules bound even the elites who weren't total bastards that thoroughly, the rules needed to change.

Fortunately for Kit, while she couldn't be rejuvenate, she could be given cybernetic replacements. And so she was. Perhaps due to her concussion, Kit remembered that in her favorite comic universe, cyborgs were considered superheros. It didn't matter if their cybernetics didn't grant superpowers, or had special gadgets either. Thus, Kit decided she was now someone qualified to be a superhero, and so she chose to try and make a difference for her city. This decision persisted after her concussion faded.

Her first approach was direct. Several years of stealth-and-gadget based vigilantism—costumed (metal) fist to the face of evil, as she cheerfully describes it—produced no systemic change whatsoever. Bad people, it turned out, had a very large recruitment pool. Far larger than one driven woman could deal with by simply punching them all until they stopped trying. She needed scale. She needed resources. Fortunately for her, there was the Terran Protectorate, a neighboring stellar empire which everyone knew about, but seemingly didn't care to interact with her people except on a conversational basis. Kit decided to find out why they were allowing oppression and tyranny to rule. For that, she needed a ship.

Finding one was easy. She was already aware of an experimental high-speed cargo and messenger ship being developed by the Gelsorft Corporation (a megacorp responsible for most of her home world's infrastructure) due to stopping several executives who were embezzling public funds to further the ship's development. So she waited for the ship to be ready for its first space flight, broke into the lab, and stole it. It wasn't easy, she didn't get away cleanly, but she made it into space and past the orbital defense canons with enough medical supplies to patch herself up and just barely enough for her new ship's own wounds.

The ship she stole was not a mere machine. It was a cybernetic organism, a genetically engineered organic creature fused to the ship's core systems as it grew and developed to serve as its computer and integral architecture. Its name, in so far as it had one, was K.A.T.. K.A.T. had not expected a thief. Kit had not expected the ship to self-identify as a woman and have goals and desires of her own. The early dynamic was rocky in the specific way that two people who have each fundamentally surprised the other by existing tend to be rocky. They worked through it, discovered they were rather fond of one another, and have since married. (Terrans will happily marry anything to anything, and not just to spite 'primitive' cultures and governments.)

Kit's plan was to fly directly to Terran space and ask the Protectorate, pointedly, why a post-scarcity utopia wasn't doing more for her people. To her surprise, her demands to talk were answered instantly by Central Command itself, the AI system that serves as the Protectorate's government (assuming that you could replace an entire government with your "cool aunt".). Central's answer was patient and thorough: unilateral intervention, however well-intentioned, creates dependency and an uneven power dynamic. The Protectorate's citizens want friends, not vassals. As such, direct unilateral intervention is saved for more extreme situations, such as wholesale genocide of popualtions. Something Kit's government wasn't doing.

Howeaver, before Kit could even be disappointed and assume the Protectorate simply wouldn't help at all, Central made her an offer. They would happily provide Kit with tactical training, technical upgrades for K.A.T. to make her something Cholli science could only dream of (same for Kit's own hardware), and an essentially unlimited supply of material and equipment for Kit to use "however she saw fit" which "Totally isn't coming from us. You found it in a derelict ship or something, right? Right. Now be sure to use all of that responsibly and absolutely not for anything like, say, starting a rebellion.". Even if Kit hadn't been a master of subtext, that wasn't the kind of statement even an idiot could misinterpret.

The blockade running years were unglamorous. Supplies in, intelligence out. Slow patient accumulation of resources, relationships, and trust. Like building a brick wall around one's house on minimum wage. It was during this period that Bar found her. Bar, short for Barbra, was a Terlix mercenary who heard about this gutsy little Chollan's plans and decided, in her own words "That sounds like a good time!". Bar joined the operation. The trio of Kit, K.A.T., and Bar became a powerful force indeed.

The liberation of Theril III ('s southern continent) began in the asteroid belt of the Theril system, where the budding rebellion had established its base of operations beyond the planetary government's easy reach. The opening phase was years of hit-and-run drop-pod in, drop-ship out orbital insertions. These commando raids had high loss rates, and were a form of grinding attritional warfare designed to weaken the planetary defenses until a crack formed. That crack came after two years of constant effort. A single point on the planet's surface in the middle of the southernmost continent. After a major operation was able to exploit that crack to establish a beachhead, the war shifted from attrition to conquest.

Kit, K.A.T., and Bar participated as commandos rather than commanders. A choice on their part despite the Rebellion wanting them as leaders. in their minds they simply didn't have the qualifications for leading a traditional war, but they could certainly make a difference on the ground. Kit had set the dominoes up. Other people helped plan where they should fall, and still others and pushed them over. The rebellion was a team effort, Kit was a cog in the machine she had built, and considered this correct.

The beachhead became a years-long campaign. The planetary government, exhausted and resource-depleted, made the calculation that they could not continue to fight and maintain control of their remaining population simultaneously, and chose to request reinforcements from the homeworld and wait out the eight year transit time. To this end they agreed to a ceasefire with the rebels. Unfortunately for the Star Empire, they had not fought any major space battles yet and had yet to lean an important lesson. Namely, without cloaking technology, there is stealth in space.

The rebellion controlled the asteroid belt. Theril III was within the belt, and the Theril system's orbital plane was aligned such that any reinforcements would be coming in parallel to it rather than perpendicular. They saw the relief fleet coming with several light years of advance notice, and forwarded targeting data to the planetary government along with a countdown of the year and a half until those ships would be in range. Cholli spaceflight depends on suspended animation to get around their lack of FTL. Not a one of those ships would be able to wake its crew in time to avoid being blown to bits via a full belt broadside. The planetary government, knowing they needed those reinforcements for their own long term stability as well as crushing the rebellion, surrendered. The southern continent of Theril III was now its own independent nation.

The rebels asked the Terran Protectorate if they would guarantee the new nation's security once a formal agreement with the Empire was in place. The Protectorate are always happy to help when asked, provided they are not being asked to do everything. Ensuring friends get to "keep their house" is well within the realm of things they'll help with.

Kit's file reached the Protectorate's Central Command around this time, flagged with a note to all Terran Immortals that read, in essence, "consider hiring this one". One such Immortal, Sam of Sol, after conducting interviews, agreed with Central's assessment, and offered the three a job. Kit and K.A.T. now operate as agents under Sam's direction, which means they go where they're needed and do what needs doing, with the full weight of the Protectorate behind them and the freedom to apply their own judgment about how.

Relationships

K.A.T.

Kit and K.A.T. are equal partners in every meaningful sense. They make decisions together, neither leads, and neither would describe the other as merely supporting the other. This dynamic is a negotiated arrangement between them, and came from their efforts to get along in the early days of their relationship. Kit needed a ship. K.A.T. needed freedom and respect. Both provided what the other needed and discovered somewhere in the process that they had also found they gave each other a lot of what they wanted. They were married in the Protectorate decades ago and remain happy to this day.

Bar

Bar is Kit's best friend, chosen sister, and the third point of the trio that liberated a continent. She arrived mid-way through the blockade running years, heard about what this gutsy little Chollan was attempting, assessed the situation personally, and decided it sounded like a good time. This is consistent with how Bar makes most life decisions. Especially the major ones.

Bar's affection for Kit is total in the way that only Terlix friendship is. Terlix make friends rarely and slowly, and the friends they do make become family in every functional sense. Bar treats Kit with closeness that other species tend to misread as romantic. This is extremely understandable given that it exceeds most species' conception of platonic love by a considerable margin. Despite this, her relationship with Kit is not romantic, nor sexual, nor in any way intrudes on Kit's marriage. Because Kit asked Bar not to. A request from a friend may as well be a divine order as far as a Terlix cares. This is easier to explain once you have seen how a Terlix couple actually operates, at which point the distinction between "Terlix romantic partner" and "Terlix friend" becomes immediately obvious. One is a gestalt entity of two people. The other is a friendship with individuals you can separate without loosing most of their personalities, goals, drives, and passions.

Bar would, and has, done anything for Kit. Kit returns this without reservation. K.A.T. considers Bar family, which among other things means Bar has unlimited docking privileges and is never charged for repairs. Especially since K.A.T. thinks Bar's ship is cute.

The Cholli

Kit is a legendary hero to the Cholli of the liberated southern continent and, to varying degrees, to Cholli everywhere. She is humble about this, maintaining that anyone who put in the same work could have done the same thing. Her people disagree and have named several things after her. She finds this embarrassing in a way she would never say out loud because it would sound ungrateful.

The Cholli Star Empire, by contrast, has classified her as a terrorist and authorized its agents to kill her on sight. Kit is aware of this. She considers it a reasonable position for them to hold given what she did, and takes appropriate precautions when operating near Imperial space.

Sam

Kit's relationship with Sam is new enough that its shape is still forming. What she knows is that Sam is a Terran Immortal; one of the Protectorate's most trusted independent agents, given essentially unlimited freedom to act because they have earned it absolutely. She knows that Sam looked at her file and said yes to their boss's request to hire her. Kit finds this simultaneously flattering and slightly terrifying, which she suspects is exactly how Sam intended it. She is learning. Sam, presumably, is watching her learn. It is the beginning of what Kit expects will be a very interesting working relationship, and Kit has learned to trust her expectations about people.

Despite this, outside of hiring interviews and a couple briefings, Kit has not spent any further time with her new boss. She's happy to carry out their orders, which so far has been simple. "Run this cargo", "capture this vessel", "chart this sector", "negotiate with these people". Nothing along the lines of what she's accomplished on her own. But Kit's not dumb. She sees the pattern in her assignments and recognizes them for what they are. A lengthy assessment to determine how she handles things. Work and a test in one. Kit is very curious to learn what she'll be doing after she passes the test.

Beliefs

Theology

Kit is a practitioner of the Breath of Choll, the animistic faith of her people. She's a sincere believer as she grew up with the religion and never found a reason to leave it behind. She does not argue about it. She does not evangelize. She simply believes that Choll (and every other planet) is alive, that all living things are an expression of that life, and that returning to the cycle at the end is the correct and natural thing to do. She finds this comforting rather than morbid, which is the intended effect as the Breath was mostly created as a way to make peace with mortality.

The most notable current debate within her faith that effects her is the machine soul question. Her specific position is both deeply considered and deeply personal. Kit holds that synthetic beings have machine souls and a machine afterlife of their own; a parallel destination rather than a shared one. The exception, in her theology, is K.A.T., whose hybrid nature she believes grants her access to both. Kit finds genuine comfort in the certainty that K.A.T. will get to see everyone, organic and synthetic alike, when all is said and done. She is quietly sad that most of her synthetic friends (of which she has many) will not be part of her own afterlife, which she accepts as the cost of loving people who are different.

Her beliefs have been challenged by the more technologically advanced Terran Protectorate's Experimental Theologians (a type of scientist who study the supernatural after having finally found empirical evidence of such things). They maintain that all consciousness, organic and synthetic, converge in the same place after death. Kit finds this interesting but not compelling. Likely due to not knowing the afterlife is a place that can be visited with a correctly calibrated wormhole, and the entire way Terran Immortals are immortal is their souls can be fetched from said afterlife to be put into a new body. Though to be fair, that is classified information.

Politics

Kit's political beliefs are simple enough to state and difficult enough to achieve that she spent the better part of her life working on them. She believes ordinary people deserve more than they are usually given. She believes systems that prevent this from changing are worth changing regardless of the cost. She believes the most effective way to change them is to build something better alongside them and let people choose.

She has no particular ideology beyond this. She is not a communist or a capitalist or an anarchist or anything that requires a pamphlet. She is someone who looked at what her society offered the average person and decided it wasn't enough, and then did something about it. The thing she chose felt obvious and right for that situation, and she wouldn't not tell you that it is the right choice for everyone everywhere. Because it wasn't. The something she did happened to work to solve a specific problem for specific people in a specific place. She considers this evidence that the approach was correct, not that she was special.

Her views remain unchanged despite her current state as a citizen of a post-scarcity and post-labor Utopian galactic scale civilization. It's simply that she's not sure what better thing you could build to let people choose between the Protectorate and, well, whatever the hell it would be. Interestingly enough, working towards that "better yet still" is exactly what Central commissioned her to do by way of working for one of the Immortals.

Personal Philosophy

Kit believes in people. Specifically, she believes in the right people, found and held onto, working together toward something worth doing. She modeled it on the comic book heroes she grew up reading, and tested it against reality for decades. It held. The friends she made are the reason she won. The friends she made are the reason she kept going when winning seemed unlikely. She does not consider this a coincidence or a lucky accident. She considers it the fundamental operating principle of the universe, and she runs her life accordingly.

She has no idea why some people call her Twilight Sparkle as a nickname. Please don't tell her. It's adorable and hilarious.

Kit has not been changed by everything she has been through. In her eyes, she tested her hypothesis and the experiment confirmed it. The Kit who decided in a hospital bed that her society needed to change is recognizably the same person as the Kit who watched a planetary government surrender. Her deepest desire is to be able to test that hypothesis enough times to write a scientific paper on her beliefs that others might follow in her footsteps.

Current Status

Kit is currently active in the field as an agent under the Terran Immortal, Sam of Sol, operating alongside K.A.T. and Bar on assignments that have so far read, to Kit's experienced eye, as a lengthy practical assessment. Cargo runs, vessel captures, sector charting, negotiations. Nothing she hasn't done before, nothing that pushes her limits, everything carefully chosen to show Sam how she handles things when left to her own judgment. Kit is aware of the pattern. She is curious rather than impatient about what comes after she passes.

Working for someone else after years of being her own boss is a quieter adjustment than she expected. It is not that she objects to taking direction. Kit has always been comfortable as a cog in a larger machine when the machine is pointed at something worth doing. It is more that she is still learning the shape of this particular machine, and Sam is not the sort of person whose shape is easily decerned, nor is their boss (and thus Kit's big boss) something that the mortal mind can easily understand.

The liberated southern continent of Theril III remains her home base. She and K.A.T. maintain a ground facility there between assignments, which means that every time she returns from the field she lands in the nation she spent the better part of her life building. She does not find this heavy. She finds it grounding in the most literal sense — a reminder of what the work is for and what it can produce when the right people commit to it together. The nation is stable. The Protectorate's security guarantee holds. The people there are building something. Kit considers this a satisfactory outcome and is already thinking about the next one.
