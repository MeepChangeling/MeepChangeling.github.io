Codex

Name: Chollan (Plural: Cholli)
Sexes: "Monosex" (homoecious).
Height: 140 - 147 cm (4'6" - 4'10").
Weight: 40 - 49 kg (88 - 108 lbs.).
Hair: White, beige, straw, or eggshell.
Eyes: Amber, yellow, or brown (Rarely orange)
Ears: Small, pointed, close to head, easily missed even if not covered by hair.
Skin: Leaf green. Other skin colors indicate medical problems as pigmentation comes from Chlorophyll. Chollan skin is notably tough, rubbery, somewhat slick, and compresses to the touch like memory foam.

Appearance

Cholli are a short, remarkably human looking, plantoid species which do not appear to be plants to the naked eye. The biggest clue is their skin, which is universally leaf green due to the presence of chlorophyll. Aside from this singular trait and a lack of sweat, there is no way to tell a Chollan apart from a mammal without a medical examination.

Like many plants Cholli are a homoecious species, their individuals posses all equipment necessary to reproduce with any other individual of their species (Though unlike many plants they cannot reproduce without a partner.), and while they do not feed their young milk they do store sap and water in specialized glands which are used for nourishing their offspring. These glands are breast-like enough for mammals to call them breasts, thereby annoying biologists of all species. Regardless of terminology, a Chollan appears very much as a short, green skinned human woman.

A Chollan's skin is almost synthetic in appearance, notably without blemishes, resilient, smooth, hairless, and somewhat squishy and springy. It is often mistaken for latex, silicon, or some other form or rubber, but is in fact a form of hydrenchyma, the same type of plant tissue which composes a cactus's stem. Internally Cholli are quite similar to certain species of cacti, notably those with wooden "skeletons".

A close examinination of a Chollan's head will show their hair to be a fine silk-like root structure. These roots are specially adapted for harvesting moisture from the air and anything they come in contact with. The rate at which they absorb water is such that a Chollan can use them to drink a liter of water within 30 seconds by immersing their head into a container. An adaptation found on many plants from their homeworld.

The Chollan eye is somewhat primitive for an intelligent lifeforms. They lack any equivalent to cone cells, which limits them to a "black and white" perception of infrared radiation. Many Cholli opt for cybernetic augmentation after learning of "color" from passing aliens, as they truly have no equivalent concept and such a thing sounds eldritch yet alluring.

Diet and Predator Mentality

Cholli are carnivorous plants which evolved to prey on the abundant animal life of their homeworld. They are Stationary Hunters and rely on their prey to approach them unawares then react with blinding speed to kill their prey in one swift move. Unlike most carnivorous plants they are not directly consuming the flesh of the their prey for nutrients. While a Cholli will eat the meat and organs of their prey, they do not have a digestive system, but rather a fermentation system. All proteins they consume are mixed with a slurry of water and sugars from various other plants and fermented into a rich ethanol product.

This fermentation does more than merely create ethanol (Cholli's direct metabolic flue). It dissolves connective tissue, breaks proteins into amino acids, destroys pathogens, produces vitamins, detoxifies most poisons, and of course, produces various alcohols. The end result of this process is the Cholli themselves spend next to no energy "Digesting" their meals, resulting in a greater effective efficiency in terms of calories in to energy out than nearly any other predator in the galaxy. As a result, their population dynamics are much closer to herbivores than carnivores.

As their bodies burn ethanol directly for their flue, they can simply drink any fermented beverage to survive short-term. Provided that beverage was fermented using their species symbiotic yeasts (or fortified with vitamins and minerals), they can survive on an industrial produced beverage their entire life in good health. Though not in good spirits.

While a Chollan could live their entire life exclusively on fortified beer produced entirely from plants, their innate instincts drive them to consume flesh, causing depression, anxiety, and bouts of rage if they are unable to hunt for substantial periods of time. Interestingly, they do not need to kill to sate these urges, only engage in their natural hunting behaviors. Various sports can take the place of hunting, though unless the Chollan is occasionally fermenting some of their own food internally, playing a sport will eventually stop providing the feeling of having hunted they need.

Most Cholli subsist on fermented plant beverages, play a sport, and occasionally purchase small amounts of meat to consume as their normal mode of existence. They do not see this as a lifestyle, but a diet. To them, the process of hunting (or an equivalent activity) is part of what is needed to survive and is also something they consume. Leading to phrases like "Could go for a hunt" being equivalent to "COuld go for a pizza".

Plantoid Anatomy, Abilities, and Limitations

Cholli are impressive for plantoids in that they are faster than most animals. In terms of swinging a limb they are capable of rapid burst movement faster than the eye can see. They are able to sprint in excess of 190 kph (118 mph) for up to 12 seconds, and can sustain a 45 kph (27 mph) run for 10-20 minutes depending on physical fitness and stored ethanol.

Cholli are capable of remaining entirely still for as long as they like. This is not a trait shared by other plants on their homeworld, all of which are motile in one way or another, and none of which ever stop moving, though may be in motion very slowly. Cholli, on the other hand, can become as static as a rock, with even internal cellular processes going into a "low-power mode" and slowing as well. While not quite a natural stasis system the Cholli exploit this for their pre-FTL seller travel by simply "suspending" themselves for 15 years at a time with no ill effects. While "suspended" a Chollan will be entirely unaware of the passage of time, unless an animal approaches them, which will remove them from suspension and will send them into fight or flight depending on their subconscious opinion as to if they can kill and eat that animal or not.

Cholli produce a specialized sap used to nourish their young before they can hunt on their own. This sap is thicker and more surgery than their blood. It can be consumed by adult Cholli as well, and acts as a super food in small amounts. Larger amounts can lead to health problems, including the death of their symbiotic yeast colony. It can also be used to patch holes in their skin if smeared onto a wound, and is one of the few substances that can promote their flesh to heal.

Cholli are highly resistant to puncture and blunt force, but can be cut as easily as most plant species. Cuts are exceptionally dangerous as their sap is very thin and does not clot nor coagulate due to its high ethanol content. As a result, without medical attention, a Cholli will bleed to death rapidly. Most Cholli carry bandages and clotting agents on them at all times to use on themselves and others. Especially since if a Cholli's bleeding stops before they bleed out (rare but possible) their flesh doesn't heal in the same way an animals will and will quickly begin to dry out then rot at the edges of any cut. Medical attention can prevent this and even reunite the flesh. Without medical attention the rot can set in and cost the Chollan a limb, or even their life.

Cholli are from a high gravity world, and are surprisingly strong in terms of sustained force but their plantoid bodies are not good at burst force (at least, not on the scale of other high-grav species). Their burst-strength is on par with humans, but they are able to keep exerting pressure much longer. For example, the strongest Chollan known holds a dead lift record of 510 kg (1,124.36 lb), on par with the pre-global augmentation human record. However, while pre-augmentation humans could hold that weight for mere moments, a Chollan can hold any weight they can lift for several minutes before fatigue sets in. They are capable of lifting and carrying their own body weight nearly effortlessly, and consequently, can climb any surface they can get a grip on and are often seen carrying equipment for other species as their primary constraint will be bulk, not mass.

Environs Typically Inhabited

Cholli are native to the planet Choll, an especially arid moon orbiting a gas giant in the Velin System. Choll's planetary classification is T4-A2-G4-H2-S4, making it close to standard for a homeworld. The notable exceptions being the G4 and T4 ratings. Choll's local gravity is 1.57g, making it inhospitable to most lifeforms long term, though visit-able for brief periods. Its temperature rating of T4, on the other hand, is likely to keep all non-desert evolved life far from its 47 - 48 C days. Which, due to light reflected from its planet, the particularities of its and its companion's orbit, and the twin stars of the Velin System, are endless.

Cholli do not have difficult adapting to lower gravity, a trait common to plantoid species. Unlike most plantoids, they do not rely on higher tempatres for any metabolic processes, and can function comfortably anywhere water will remain liquid. As such they are happy to colonize nearly any planet most other species would also like to colonize. This has yet to cause any strife as they lack FTL technology and have only colonized their neighboring 8 star systems, prioritizing planets with high amounts of water, which they harvest and ship back to Choll as part of ongoing efforts to create a sea.

Cholli who leave their budding seller empire to explore the stars typically develop an affinity for human space habitats. The specific light wavelengths humans prefer to use for illumination effect Cholli much the same as antidepressants, and they tend to find the human preferred temperature range to be "relaxing". As the Cholli are carnivorous plants, once exposed to burgers, it is very difficult to get one to leave a human settlement.

Lifespan and Aging

Cholli are long lived by the standards of most sapient species, a trait they share with most plantoitds. A Chollan in good health, on a world that isn't trying to kill them, can expect to live somewhere between two and three centuries naturally. Aging in a Chollan is, appropriately for a plant, a process of lignification. The woody skeleton that sits beneath their famously rubbery skin begins to express itself outward over time. Skin that was soft and springy in youth gradually hardens, dries, and takes on a texture closer to bark than rubber. Their hair becomes coarser and less efficient at drawing moisture from the air, creating a feedback loop. Less moisture absorbed means drier tissue, which means less efficient absorption still. A Chollan petrifies with age. Slowly, from the outside in. Movement becomes less fluid as their flexibility wanes, their tissues hardening incrementally over decades until a very old Chollan moves with a cadence eerily similar to early bipedal robots, and a lot of pain noises.

The end stage of a Chollan life is essentially mummification. The body desiccates rather than decays, which is why Chollan burial rites developed around returning the body to the ecosystem for consumption. A desiccated elder returned to the soil gives back what water remains in their tissues, and whatever animals can make use of their lignified flesh do so. It is the most water-efficient thing a Chollan can do with a body on a world that has never had much to spare.

Rejuvenation technology exists and can arrest or reverse this process entirely, resetting a Chollan's biology to a younger state and extending their lifespan indefinitely. It is also strictly controlled by the Cholli Star Empire, available exclusively to the ruling elite and their favored associates within the elite caste. It cannot be gifted to a non-elite recipient under any circumstances regardless of what they may have done to deserve it. The Empire's position is that access to extended life is a privilege of station. Most ordinary Cholli on the homeworld try not to think about this too hard. Those on the colonies may already be in the early stages of rebellion over the technology's restricted nature.

Life Cycle

Cholli reproduce sexualy in a process comparable to most animal species. This is due to their gametes being suspended in a sweet sap which attracts and nourishes a symbiotic yeast that aids in the development of their young. Once fertilized a Chollan seals her pistil and uses a mixture of fermentation and germination to create one to three bulbs. These bulbs are roughly the size of an orange and resemble a clove of garlic so closely that it was very easy for their government to convince most Chollans that humans like to eat their babies.

Cholli are a remarkably pragmatic people, with a religion that empathizes the importance of allowing nature to take its course with one's physical remains. As a result, they do not mind if they are eaten post-death. Nor do they mind if their bodies are used for industrial purposes. Indeed, Cholli themselves make use of the bones of their ancestors in many consumer goods. Notably weapon handles and grips, as using someone's bones in this way is seen as a means of honoring them as it allows the departed to aid the living in a sense.

Since they do not object (usually) their flesh has been sold to aliens for consumption on several occasions. Herbivores tend to find them unpalatable, notably spicy, bitter, and acrid. Omnivores tend to find them quite delicious, with many comparing them to various pickled vegetables from their homeworlds. Humans who have tried Chollan flesh insist they taste like beer battered pickles, a universal viewpoint among those who try them.

Due to a Chollan bulb being indistinguishable from an "unusually large" clove of white garlic, humans (and their allies) have, occasionally, eaten young Cholli. Their flavor is also universal to the human palate, and comes across as "Roast apples and tequila." While these events have been accidents (Cholli tend to store their bulbs in cool, dry places prior to growing them. Which often resemble pantries.) they have caused extreme amounts of friction.

Despite their views on their dead being used as food, Cholli strongly object to being killed to feed someone else. As one would expect, given their cultures and mainstream religion is only pushing for a traditional burial which ensures their body will be used in a natural way post-mortem. Consequently, they strongly and violently oppose predators.

Cholli are laied by their mother as a bulb. Much like an egg, this bulb needs to be incubated. The incubation process requires warmth, moisture, and limited light. Unlike eggs a Cholli bulb is shelf stable and can be incubated at any point in its future so long as it doesn't rot, nor extensively damaged by pests (provided 40% of the bulb is intact, it can grow into a healthy child. Many parents split bulbs in half if they desire twins.)

A Chollan child grows under the surface, with only their roots exposed, for 8-13 months. They will dig themselves free as soon as they become conscious, which is not necessarily the same moment their body is finished developing. Keeping a mid-developed Chollan burred is next to impossible without injuring them. Their medical science only recently found a safe means of putting them back to sleep for development. Consequently parents are still often employing the traditional development of quickly and humanly killing any pre-mature 'surfacing'.

Once developed and on the surface, a young Chollan develops fairly slowly, reaching sexual maturity in 28 years, and are considered fully grown in 32 years. Albeit their culture's age of adulthood tends to be between 22 and 27 years as that is the rough time it takes for a Chollan to become mentally mature and useful to society at large.

Inter-species Relations

The Cholli Star Empire, the primary state in control of Chollan territory, is best described as a xenophobic fascist regime pretending to be a people's republic, despite calling itself an Empire. This is due to the term "empire" not having the same connotations to Cholli as it does most other species. They would define an empire as something more akin to a republic "but with a guy in charge". There is likely a better term to translate their word "Jieia" to other than "Empire" but their government's official policy of "minimal contact only" for other species makes such things slow at best, and impossible at worst.

The Cholli Star Empire is terrified of the galaxy around them. Very few species advance to the interstellar stage (with or without FTL) in the same type of social state the Cholli have. While there are plenty of kingdoms and empires leaving their homeworlds, these are most always constitutional monarchies. The Cholli Star Empire is an absolute monarchy quite similar to Imperial Japan in its general structure, and makes use of racial divisions within the Cholli species as its justification for why their elites are elites. These types of governments historically do not do well post first contact as racial supremacy within a species typically dies as soon as they find other sapient life which tends to cause a species with such a culture to unify under "Even our worst is better than those damn xenos!" which causes internal friction that results in regime change.

This is a fact that the Cholli Star Empire's spies were able to pull from a diplomat's computer during first contact with the Terran Protectorate.

In the Cholli's case, the effect is worse. Individual Chollan do not tend to see themselves as better than aliens. This is due to the racial superiority they experience in their culture being based on that race's accomplishments rather than innate qualities. For example the Cluuthi, the race which includes current Emperor, is Top Dog in the racial hierarchy because the Emperor's ancestor unified the Challi people into a singular government, and previously, also developed their internet, rejuvenation technology, and digital watches. Any accomplishment an individual makes brings glory to their race, and should a race be glorious enough in the eyes of others, its members are seen as more important than others.

Most governing officials and elites are so out of touch with their people that they are unable to see the average Chollan on the street looks at aliens as people playing an entirely different game. It doesn't matter to them that aliens have FTL and they don't. The Cholli's game is separate from their game.

The officials who are aware of the general public sentiment are also aware fo the true problem. A Chollan exposed to a more typical culture suddenly becomes aware that all of these mighty civilizations who can do so much more than their own, do not glorify the group due to an individuals' achievements. Rather, they glorify the individual. This tends to make the average Chollan very angry as the overwhelming majority of them are incredibly hard working in the hopes of advancing their caste's standing. Had their efforts been tabulated on the xeno's scale, they would likely be far better off than they are now.

Thus far, all Cholli who have left their worlds for alien ones have not returned, but have done their best to spread the word of what they see as a much more fair system. This terrifies the Cholli Star Empire and has led to them repressing and restricting everything they can to keep their grip on power.

This irritates the hell out of their singular neighbor, the Terran Protectorate, who is well known to have "a thing" for little green alien women, and also generally dislikes tyrannical regimes who would rather let their people starve than accept donated food.

Thus far war has been averted mostly due to the Protectorate not wanting to cause civilian casualties and being creatively certain the Empire will collapse within 50 years, leaving an opportunity to swoop in and be the good neighbor they like to be. As well as the existence of the Cholli Mafias, which are bizarrely formal within Cholli society and stand a chance of supplanting the Empire's rule without the Empire going away.

Cholli Mafias are more than mere organized crime syndicates. They frequently hold territory within a region, effectively acting as a county or occasionally city government. Occasionally a Mafia might even be officially the ruling body of a given region. In these cases it is usually due to the Mafia having been able to take and hold that territory by force, or having filled a administrative vacuum the Empire was too thinly spread to fill itself.

Where any given Mafia came from is, depending on who you ask, a completely different answer. Some families trace their origins to revolutionary movements that formed in direct opposition to Imperial overreach. Others claim descent from merchant guilds that accumulated enough leverage to start writing their own rules. Others still began as straightforward criminal enterprises that grew into something with more structure and ambition than their founders intended. One definitively started as one woman's effort to crush her enemies, drive them before her, and hear the lamentations of their women; since her enemies were an HoA, no one particularity cared to stop her. None of these origin stories are mutually exclusive, several families claim the same founding myths, and at least two are definitely lying about theirs. The honest answer is that the Mafias as a collective institution do not have a single origin, and any account that says otherwise is either talking about one specific family or trying to sell you something.

That said, some blanket statements can be made about the Mafias.

Membership is earned rather than inherited. A Chollan belongs because they proved themselves useful, trustworthy, or capable enough to be brought in. What that proof looks like varies considerably by family. Some have formal trials or tests. Others simply watch a person over time and extend an invitation when they've seen enough. Being born into a Mafia family gives you proximity and opportunity, but not membership itself (with a few exceptions). This is considered one of the more meritocratic aspects of Chollan society, and is a source of envy for most of their people.

Internal structure varies as much as origin. Some families run on strict hierarchy with clear ranks and a chain of command that would satisfy a military officer. Others are effectively flat, with leadership going to whoever is best at leading in a given situation. Most fall somewhere between these extremes and have evolved their structure organically over generations rather than designed it deliberately. The one universal is that a Mafia leader who stops being effective stops being a leader, by one means or another. Traditionally by mysterious circumstances after which they are never seen again.

Between families, there is a loose network of understood rules rather than formal cooperation. Families do not generally work together, and open hostility between them is not unheard of. There are lines that are simply not crossed. Certain territories are respected, certain people are off limits, certain kinds of collateral damage are unacceptable. These rules are unwritten, unenforced by any authority, and surprisingly consistent. Violating them always brings the kind of consequences that don't require a court.

The Empire's relationship with the Mafias is complicated. Some families hold their territories under formal treaties, the terms of which are a matter of public record and rather boring. The actual value of the arrangement runs underneath that. Recognized Families provide the Empire with a useful party to blame when something goes wrong in a way the Empire would prefer not to own, a channel for activities that cannot be put on Imperial letterhead, and kickbacks that are never referred to as kickbacks. In exchange they receive legitimacy, protection, and the Empire's blind eye.

The Mafias collectively control nearly a third of the homeworld at present. The Terran Protectorate watches this with considerable interest, having noted that a parallel power structure with popular legitimacy and territorial control is exactly the kind of institution that could absorb Imperial authority without requiring the Empire to visibly collapse first. Much like the Shogunate did with Feudal Japan. The Empire has also noted this. It is why their relationship with even the allied families has a certain careful quality to it, the way you might be careful around something you need but cannot fully trust.

On the individual level, Cholli have no issue mingling with humans at all. They are too large for the average Chollan to think of as prey, and most of them are very much happy to engage in the most treasured of Cholli pastimes, having a cold one and watching the game. As far as humans care, they've stumbled onto an entire species of small green-skinned tomboys, a literal dream come true for their pre-interstellar ancestors. While less than 3000 have left their nation behind, all of them are well loved parts of various terran communities. No yet known species has a greater integration quotient for humans themselves. Though there is some friction with other species within the Terran umbrella, and about a third of Cholli appear to have issues with AI and android Terrans due to not believing machines can have souls.

It is noteworthy that Cholli, as a whole, seem to have a romantic attraction to humans. While the effect is subtle compared to many species which find other species attractive, preliminary studies indicate they can, will, and do form stable long term romantic relationships with humans. A noteworthy occurrence as while inter-species couples can be found everywhere and cover every species combination in existence, species-wide attraction is rare.

Government

The Cholli Star Empire is an absolute monarchy administered in practice by a layered system of noble houses and professional bureaucrats, in roughly the same way a wedding cake is supported by its tiers and the table beneath it. The noble houses provide the weight and the ceremony. The bureaucrats do the actual work of keeping it from collapsing.

Noble rank within the Empire is tied directly to a clan's accumulated glory — the sum total of great things members of that clan have contributed to Chollan civilization. The Cluuthi sit at the top of this hierarchy because their ancestor unified the Cholli under a single government, and before that, gave them the internet, rejuvenation technology, and digital watches. This is considered sufficient. Whether the current Cluuthi are doing anything similarly impressive is, officially, not a relevant question. In practice, most noble houses have been coasting on their ancestors' achievements for generations, a fact that sits rather awkwardly alongside a system that is theoretically meritocratic.

The throne itself is, in theory, challengeable. Any clan who believes their accumulated glory now surpasses the Emperor's may file a Deed of Surpassing — a formal public declaration that is then put to a council of noble houses to weigh and vote on. In theory, this means the Empire's leadership is always its most accomplished. In practice, the council is composed entirely of the same old money houses who benefit from the current arrangement, and a legitimate challenger will find their Deed buried in procedural review until they either give up or become politically dangerous enough that denial would be more embarrassing than acceptance. The mechanism exists. It has never worked cleanly.

The relationship between the Empire and the Cholli Mafias is best described as complicated in the way that a parasite and its host are complicated. Certain Mafia families hold formal treaties with the Empire — their territorial control is officially recognized in exchange for kickbacks, the occasional favor that can't be put in writing, and the very useful service of being available to take the blame when something goes wrong in a way the Empire would prefer not to own. The Empire gets a shadow arm that operates outside its official laws and a convenient scapegoat. The Mafias get legitimacy and protection. Everyone involved understands exactly what this arrangement is and refers to it in the most formal possible language.

The Mafias which are not party to such treaties are, from the Empire's perspective, simply unrecognized criminals. From the Protectorate's perspective, they are the most interesting political development in the region. A Mafia with enough territory, enough popular support, and enough patience could theoretically supplant Imperial authority without replacing Imperial structure — much as the Shogunate functioned within Feudal Japan. The Empire is aware of this possibility. It is one of several things keeping their intelligence services very busy.

Religion

The dominant faith of the Cholli people is called The Breath of Choll, or simply The Breath in casual usage. It is an animistic faith in the philosophical sense rather than the literal one. Spirits are used as allegorical language for the forces and cycles that govern all living things, with Choll itself as the central sacred concept. The planet is not merely where the Cholli are from. It is, in the theological sense, alive. The conventional position is everything that lives upon it is a "cell" of Choll itself, and the natural cycles of life are the planet's breath.

The core of their religion treats the natural cycle of consumption with something approaching reverence. Everything eats. Everything is eaten. Nothing is wasted. Death is not a tragedy to be mourned but the fundamental sacred truth of existence. Cholli burial rites involve preparing the body for consumption by animals, with any other useful parts being given new life in some form.

The Breath is organized around a formal church (born of an ancient order of philosophers and their disciples) with notable political weight. Its clergy are among the better educated members of Chollan society, and always have been. The church has historically controlled a significant portion of Chollan record-keeping, scholarship, and schools. This is its primary basis of power, with its secondary being Emperors require the church's blessing to be crowned. The church has only once officially refused to crown an emperor, and seems unwilling to do so again due to the resulting civil war. Though it has, in the present, been bizarrely slow to grant permission in the hopes the current situation would change.

The relationship between The Breath and the Cholli Star Empire is one of mutual dependence and mutual suspicion. The Empire needs the church's legitimacy. The church needs the Empire's protection and resources. Neither fully trusts the other, and both are correct not to. In practice, the church's primary form of dissent is quiet and deniable: Sheltering Chollans who have run afoul of the Empire in ways the clergy consider unjust, maintaining records the Empire would prefer lost, and occasionally misplacing paperwork at inconvenient moments.

One area of genuine ongoing theological debate within The Breath is the question of machine souls. A debate which began shortly after First Contact, as the Terran diplomats brought several synthetic lifeforms with them, as Terrans are want to do. The faith's animistic framework holds that Choll breathes through living things. What constitutes a living thing is a question the faith's founders did not anticipate needing to answer. Roughly a third of Cholli, drawing on more conservative interpretations, hold that souls require biological life and that AI and android beings therefore exist outside the sacred cycle entirely. Another faction argues that anything capable of thought and growth participates in the Breath regardless of its substrate. After all, everything in an android came from the earth just like anyone else. A third major wing of the debate holds that synthetic life have synthetic souls which in turn belong to a synthetic afterlife that natural beings cannot go to any more than synthetic beings can go to the natural afterlife, and as such both are separate but equal.

This debate has not been resolved, shows no signs of being resolved, and has generated an impressive amount of very passionate philosophical literature. It is also the a source of friction between Cholli and the AI and android citizens of the Terran Protectorate (who are mostly just tired of being a theological thought experiment. Again.).

Technology

Compared to the Terran Protectorate, Cholli technology is, charitably, developing. Uncharitably, its just bairly developed enough to make interacting with them legal. The Protectorate is a post-scarcity, post-labor civilization whose most impressive engineering projects involve detonating stars and using time dilation to coax new systems into forming from the debris. The Cholli's most celebrated technological achievement is a form of rejuvenation medicine that can prolong life, restore small missing sections of a body, has occasionally healed the blind, and can (if one is swift and lucky) bring back the recently deceased. An equivalent medicine can be obtained from most vending machines in the galaxy. The Empire is aware of this in the way that a person standing at the edge of a very tall cliff is aware of the cliff.

Where the Cholli have genuinely excelled is in the fields they were forced to develop by circumstance. Their medical technology punches well above their overall developmental weight. Their understanding of biological systems, fermentation chemistry, and wound management is sophisticated enough that Terran xenobiologists find their medical literature genuinely interesting (and occasionally useful) rather than merely quaint.

Their weapons technology tells a similar story. Cholli ballistic weapons are, by a significant margin, the most advanced in the known galaxy. This is largely due to most species finding a cheep enough energy source to make energy weapons viable and abandoning ballistics, save for railguns on starships, defense platforms, and ground-to-space emplacements. The Cholli are notably behind in energy technology for their development level, and thus, eek out every last ounce of ker-blam they can from every grain of gunpowder they produce.

Their greatest technological weaknesses are energy production and propulsion. Choll is a hot, arid, high-gravity moon with no ocean, minimal tidal and hydroelectric resources, and an atmosphere that makes getting anything off the surface an expensive proposition. The Cholli response to this constraint has been characteristically pragmatic: Do more with less, or die. Their suspension ability, which allows a Chollan to slow their metabolic processes to near-nothing for extended periods, has been deliberately cultivated as a space travel solution. A Cholli interstellar expedition burns almost no resources in transit because the crew simply doesn't need life support other than minimal heating to not freeze. Fifteen years between stars costs the traveler nothing but time they weren't conscious for. It works well enough that the Cholli have reached and colonized multiple neighboring star systems with this method.

FTL travel remains out of reach, and is likely to stay that way until the energy gap closes. The Empire knows this. It is another item on a long list of reasons they find the Terran Protectorate's proximity unsettling. The Terran Protectorate, meanwhile, remains frustrated that the Cholli have everything they need to make the most simple FTL system possible (the Surf Drive) already, but just don't know it, because the individual components are held by six rival universities who refuse to colab on anything.

Language

The Cholli once spoke many languages, as most civilizations do, but the Empire's unification efforts were thorough enough that all but a handful have been eliminated. Those which remain are existent only as academic curiosities (preserved mostly because certain important ancient texts were written in them), a couple languages spoken by a handful of very remote populations, and one new language that popped up on a colony world that takes a good long time for imperial ships to reach.

The dominant language of the Cholli people is Nilric. Nilric itself is written in a system of simple carved runes, straight lines and angles that can be cut into stone, wood, or metal with minimal tooling. It is a practical writing system developed by a practical people on a world where elaborate brushwork would have been sand-blasted away before it dried.

For most alien species, Nilric is learnable but difficult. Its structure is sufficiently alien that fluency takes considerable time and the lossy quality of translation is a known problem. The exception is humans, who have little difficulty learning Nilric. Specifically, humans who speak Spanish find Nilric uncomfortably familiar. The overlap is significant enough that a native Spanish speaker and a Chollan with no formal language training can achieve rough mutual comprehension with patience and goodwill, and linguists on both sides have been arguing about why ever since first contact. It is one of those coincidences that delights humans enormously, to the point where various linguistic nerds begin spooling their FTL drives to swarm upon the novelty. However the the Cholli Star Empire has officially classified as a "mere coincidence, full stop" and prohibit studying the similarities.

The Empire's insistence on this point is not purely academic. The broader galaxy has a known history with a precursor species who seeded proto-human DNA across multiple worlds as a long-running experiment in convergent sapient development. The evidence is genomic and well documented. A significant number of species are, in a meaningful biological sense, derivatives of humanity, a fact that causes considerable distress among various racial supremacist factions across the galaxy for reasons that require very little explanation. The Cholli are not among these species. Their DNA was checked thoroughly at first contact and the precursor's fingerprints are absent. But the Spanish coincidence looks like evidence to anyone who doesn't know what to look for when examining their genetics. The Empire is acutely aware that "maybe we're related to humans after all" is exactly the kind of popular sentiment that erodes the ideological foundations they've spent centuries building.

Unfortunatly there's nothing they can do to stop their citizens from tuning into old human media broadcasts in Spanish which are "coincidentally" tight-beamed at Choll.

Weapons and Warfare

Cholli military doctrine evolved from a practical problem native to their homeworld. The animals of Choll are fast, resilient, and difficult to bring down reliably with simple weapons. The solution the Cholli arrived at over centuries of hunting and conflict was simple: Use a gun. If that don't work, use more gun. In more broad terms if you cannot guarantee the kill in the moment, you make sure the target cannot escape the kill after the moment. This philosophy saturates their entire approach to warfare.

Their preferred engagement style reflects their origins as stationary ambush hunters. Cholli forces are patient, deliberate, and oriented around controlling a situation before it begins rather than reacting to one already in progress. They do not like fair fights. A fair fight means someone made a planning error.

Cholli firearms are extraordinarily capable, featuring onboard lidar, guided munitions, predictive targeting systems that can estimate a target's position after line of sight is broken, and standard ammunition that is both toxic and contains tracking compounds. They are exceptionally more accurate, further ranged, and have more stopping power than any other ballistic weapons by far. So much so that many people consider them on par with modern energy weapons.

They are still actively developing their firearms as well. Limited engagements with alien forces saw their weapons ineffective against personal shields and the alloys found in powered armor. But only for the first six months. After which the Cholli had developed a discarding sabot round that could pierce hardlight. Two more months and they would pierce the hardlight then explode with enough force to kill anyone without impact dampening gel in their armor.

They are the galaxy's undisputed foremost experts in firearms, and have an open invite to "Range Day", the most sacred holiday of the Terran colony 'Merica'.

Their vulnerability is the mirror of their strength. Energy and thermal weapons are extremely effective against Cholli biology. They are plants, and plants burn. Most Cholli military units are acutely aware of this and plan accordingly, which usually means either closing distance fast, or controlling terrain, and ensuring the engagement never reaches the point where an opponent can bring sustained energy fire to bear. A Cholli force that lets itself get pinned in open ground against energy weapons has, again, made a planning error.

History

Chollan history revolves around water. The moon has never had enough of it. Underground reservoirs and seasonal surface flows were the difference between a settlement that survived and one that didn't, and for most of Chollan prehistory the primary occupation of any organized group was either defending access to water or trying to take it from someone else. The ambush predator instincts that served individual Cholli so well in hunting translated naturally into warfare, and the result was centuries of grinding, brutal conflict between city states and language groups that wanted the same reservoirs and weren't interested in sharing.

The Mafias emerged during this period, predating any unified government by a significant margin. Some grew from resistance movements, some from merchant guilds protecting trade routes between isolated settlements, some from straightforward criminal enterprises that developed ambitions. What they had in common was that they were functional when formal governments weren't. In those days that counted for more than legitimacy. By the time anyone thought to object to their existence they were more than load-bearing. They were the foundation.

After ages of these bloody conflicts, Unification came through the Cluuthi, a clan so powerful that their world domination felt like the inevitable hand of fate rather than an accomplishment. The Cluuthi's most celebrated ancestor gave the Cholli three things in close enough succession that history has compressed them into a single legacy: a global communications network, rejuvenation technology, and a unified government. The internet connected populations that had been isolated enough to develop entirely distinct languages. Rejuvenation technology meant the people making decisions lived long enough to see the consequences of them. And the unified government that followed was built on the argument that the Cluuthi had earned the right to run it, an argument that was difficult to counter when they were standing next to the infrastructure that made it possible. The wars did not end quickly or cleanly. Centuries of conflict do not resolve in a generation. But they ended.

The Empire that emerged consolidated slowly. The oldest Mafias were already there and couldn't be removed, so they were worked around, occasionally worked with, and when necessary officially recognized in exchange for arrangements that benefited both parties and that neither party discusses openly. The languages of the defeated city states were suppressed, with exceptions made for those needed to read important ancient documents and those spoken by populations too remote to bother with. Nilric became the tongue of a unified people whether those people had chosen it or not.

Stellar travel came centuries after unification, once the Empire had consolidated enough to pool resources toward the water problem. Namly, there was water out there. Or so the scientists said. And out there was not here. Thus, how to get it? The solution they arrived at was characteristically efficient and characteristically Chollan. Rather than solving the energy problem that made getting off a high gravity world so expensive, they minimized the cost of the journey itself. Suspension travel required almost nothing. A hibernating crew didn't need to eat, drink, or breathe at meaningful rates. Fifteen years between stars cost the travelers nothing but time they wouldn't remember. Eight systems were reached this way, colonies established, water harvested and shipped back to Choll as part of the ongoing project of giving their homeworld something it had never had enough of.

First contact with the Terran Protectorate was not the dignified moment of civilizational significance the Empire would have preferred. A lone SETI operator at a remote outpost became bored enough to aim a dish at the sky and send a message out for a change. By a staggering coincidence, she achieved contact with a passing Terran vessel that happened to be using radio communication as part of a historical reenactment. The Terrans, being Terrans who were out and about "space-trucking" to experience what "the open road" had been like for their ancestors, were immediately delighted. They responded warmly, and due to not being able to pinpoint such a low power radio transmission, simply "at-everyone'd" the whole world. With considerable enthusiasm. In passable Nilric. All before anyone in the Empire had the opportunity to contain the situation and present it as an official discovery.

The operator's name spread through human space faster than any Imperial communique could. She became, despite the Empire's considerable efforts to the contrary, a folk hero. One celebrated in human space as the woman who got to live the SETI dream, a galactic first! Unfortunately, she was regarded by the Empire as a persistent administrative inconvenience who they cannot do anything about because the Terrans won't stop talking about her. She is alive, though her current whereabouts are officially unknown. To the Empire. Her words of condemnation of the Empire, however, are not unknown, and are regulatory broadcast as a radio program to the whole of the Cholli Empire. The Empire finds this irritating. Terrans find this delightful. Most ordinary Cholli, if they have heard of her at all, find this extremely funny.
