Codex

Name: Terlex (no plural)
Sexes: Male and Female.
Height: 152 - 177 cm (5'0" - 5'10").
Weight: 58–69 kg (128–153 lbs.).
Hair: White, platinum, silver, ivory. Hair found only on scalp, eyebrows, and eye lashes.
Skin: Brown, almond, chocolate, russet, sepia. Skin is smooth and hairless.
Fur: Black, orange, bronze, tan, beige, silver. Found exclusively on the tail.
Eyes: Blue, green, orange, silver.
Ears: Large, triangular, prehensile. Express emotions.

While Terlix are a kind species and often said to be pleasant to live around, it is not recommended to visit them on their colonies, nor habitats.

Terlix are not a standard species, despite appearing much like the majority of them. They are capable of withstanding UV radiation which is deadly to the vast majority of lifeforms in the galaxy. This is due to their homeworld orbiting Kultr (Sol Reg. HR 5936 aka Lambda Coronae Borealis), a F-type main-sequence star with exceptionally high UV and x-ray output. While the x-rays are filtered enough to be a neglagable hazard, Gulat's atmosphere is entirely permeable to UVB.

A pale skinned human will experience first degree sunburn in 5-10 minutes, with darker skinned humans experiencing the same within 20-40 minutes. Second degree sunburn comes shortly after, with permanent and serious burns occurring within an hour.

Skin damage is but the tip of the iceberg. Eye damage, including the development of cataracts, occurs with extreme rapidity as local plant life reflects UV light meaning there is no safe direction one can look to avoid UV damage to the eyes if outdoors. A single day in their sun poses a greatly increased risk of skin cancer, and is likely to cause heatstroke in any non-heat adapted species.

Terlix see far into the UV spectrum, and prefer to keep their living quarters illuminated with a mixture of visible light and UV, making their quarters just as great a risk as their homeworld. They are fine in "dim lighting", but turning up to one's home unannounced poses a serious radiological hazard to most lifeforms.

Appearance

At first glance the Terlix appear to be exactly what 20th century human science fiction predicted first contact would be with. A bipedal, vaguely feline, human-like alien species that puts one into an easy going state of "Oh, aliens are just people too." But the longer one looks, the more alien a Terlix becomes. The first difference one notes is their digitigrade stance and paws. Their abdomens are about 20% longer than a human's, as are their arms. Their hands are larger with shorter yet thicker fingers tipped in retractable claws. All important for a species that highly prefers moving long distance on all fours.

Quadrapedal locomotion is their specie's norm, with much of their homes and ships built with this in mind. Bipedal species often find the hallways on such structures to be cramped, and will likely have to stoop to pass through.

Terlix have claws on their hands and paws in place of nails. These are functional natural weapons and not to be underestimated. Their claws have three primary states:

  1. Retracted: A Terlix can retract their claws entirely out of their fingers into the backs of their hands and paws. This is necessary for them to be able to move their fingers without having their claws extended. Their fingers are much more flexible in this state. Maintaining this state is a conscious effort on their part, but minimally so, like keeping a grip on a bag.
  2. Resting: At rest, a Trelix's claws extend two centimeters from their finger tips. This prevents is used when they walk or run to maximize traction, and for basic utility cutting such as skinning prey, opening mail, marking territory, ruining a cheating ex's furniture, etc.
  3. Extended: At full extension a Terlix's claws measure nearly 6cm. Their fingers are once again fully felxable, and their claws lock into their extended state much like folding knives, making them effortless to keep extended. This is for active hunting and fighting, as Terlix dislike doing much else with their claws fully out.

Terlix claws contain hematite and are capable of cutting softer metals and engraving steel. Their finger bones, ligaments, and cartilage are also quite robust, with a single finger capable of withstanding four times the force a Terlix can generate on their own. Consequently they can stab and slash with their claws without injuring themselves even if they strike solid stone with full force. Their claws rest at approximately 2 cm out of their sheathes, and average a usable length of 6cm. Fully extended, most Terlix can puncture up to 3 cm of softer metals and 4 cm of wood, and cut through up to 2cm of softer materials with a swipe of the hand or paw.

Despite this they are not able to use their claws as primary melee weapons against conventionally armored forces. A modern armor alloy's hardness far exceeds their capacity to damage material. However, if a Terlix is able to find a gap in the armor, or attacks a soft joint, they are able to cut and puncture most commonly used materials, including kevlar.

Their instinctive hunting behaviors compel them to attack joints, necks, and groins first, and so they treat their claws as natural sidearms. Consequently their martial arts and military training has up-to-date strategies, tactics, and forms for fighting with their natural weapons. A Terlix is consequently never considered unarmed by any legal system in the galaxy, nor by anyone with more than half a brain.

Despite this, the odds of being physically assaulted by a Terlix are very low as they do not see animals as prey if they are larger than 2/3rds their own size and culturally find the idea of eating another thinking being to be abhorrent. However, plenty of spacers who backed a Terlix into a corner have bled out before medical help could arrive.

A Terlix's face is thinner and more angular than a humans, especially their jaws, which can open quite wide despite being flat like an ape's. Their teeth are mostly human like in the front, through rather than molars they have pointed spike-like rear teeth that help shred meat. Their noses are shorter, flatter, and wider, like a cross between a feline and human nose.

Their skin is smooth and hairless, comparable to many aquatic mammals. It is notably tough, able to resist cuts from conventional alloy blades, though it shows no particular resistance to punctures. Their hair comes in shades of white, which contrasts with the brown tones their skin comes in and is of note when their fur tends to be black or orange.

Terlix hair color is linked directly to the average color of their homeworld's grass. All tall grasses on Gulat are a similar shade of silvery white (remenessent of car paint in terms of specular properties and glitter) due to the need for plant life to reflect UV light. Consequently, Terlix hair matches these properties, as did the majority of their ancestor's fur.

Their non-white fur on their tails is believed to have extended across their backs and inner thighs. This fur is much denser and tougher than their hair, and serves as a form of ablative armor. The Terlix's ancestors drove their predators to extinction long ago, so much so that they lost their armored fur in favor of better sweat glands, save for that covering their tails.

Interestingly while Terlix hair glows under a black light, their fur does not.

Terlix retain their camouflage due to a quirk of vision in most animals on their homeworld. They do not distinguish the color brown from various UV "colors". As such brown blends in with the plants well enough, making a Terlix appear like a shady spot rather than a creature.

Terlix are sexually dimorphic, with their females being larger, more muscular, and typically more aggressive. Their males are shorter, rounder, and "cuter" by Terlix standards. This is due to their reproductive behaviors, which see males seducing the females as a female can easily fight off most males. Older Terlix females tend to have breast-like mounds on their chests. These should not be used as a sex-distinguishing marker as they only emerge during pregnancy. Any female who has not reproduced will be as flat chested as a male. Additionally they are not mammary glands, but rather protective padding that develops within their pouches after habitation by their young. The pouch's slits are not present on males, giving them a featureless chest.

Despite the presence of a pouch, Terlix are not marsupials, nor are they mammals. Though they are close enough for most people to count them as such. The primary difference being their developing young feed on their mother's blood, which is why the pouches are near the heart and lungs rather than the abdomen. As primarily carnivores, Terlix keep a taste for blood throughout their lives and make use of it in many of their beverages and sauces.

Environs Typically Inhabited

Terlix are native to the planet Gulat, a world predominantly covered in savannas, steppes, and jungles. It is a hot world, though Terlix did not adapt to make use of heat and are thus fine in cooler environments. This is not true of UV light, specifically UVB, which Terlix vision depends on for detail and identifying prey. Gulat is an extreme UV world, with life there having adapted to its levels. Consequently, Terlix are found mostly on hot, grassy worlds around F-type stars.

Such worlds are few and far between, leading to Terlix developing space habs to suit their needs and parking them in orbit of worlds they make use of for industrial or agricultural purposes, or belong to a species they are allied with. Terlix are noteworthy one of the few pursuit predators to use burrows. They prefer to build their homes and infrastructure under the ground where it's cooler and more secure. Their dwellings and habitats are thus cramped by most species standards. They can be uncomfortable in larger indoor spaces, a relatively common phobia equivalent to agoraphobia. Notably these individuals do not suffer from a fear of the outdoors, just a fear of indoor spaces they cannot stand and touch the ceiling in.

Life Cycle

Terlix are exceptionally long lived. So much so that they may be biologically immortal. Most fall victim to wear and tear on their bodies resulting in an inability to survive a disease rather than a slow decay or genuine senescence. Many die from injury inflicted by another individual or an accident. Others by chronic health conditions like heart disease. On average, this occurs around 800 years after birth. Though plenty of Terlix live well beyond this, and some of their oldest individuals are more than a thousand years old while still appearing in their primes.

Consequently, Terlix reproduce very infrequently and while they are physically mature within 15 years, consider their children to be children for 40-50 years, depending on when the individual reaches what they consider to be mental maturity.

Females are physically dominant as a direct evolutionary consequence of the long period of vulnerability they experience during pregnancy. A pregnant Terlix's mobility drops dramatically, to the point of near paralysis at the end of their joey's development. This makes mate selection a matter of survival for their oldest ancestors. Thus, Females evolved to be highly selective and physically capable of repelling unwanted advances. Often with deadly force. Males evolved accordingly, favoring allure and persistence over aggression. The end result is females that are larger, stronger, and tend towards dominant personalities, while males are smaller, cuter, and tend towards submissive personalities.

Shockingly this dynamic is entirely confined to reproduction. Terlix society has always been functionally egalitarian in every other domain, and the concept of gender roles extending beyond their specific biological function strikes most Terlix as baffling.

This is largely due to the needs of a pregnant female. She will need feeding as she will loose the ability to hunt. She will need protecting as she will eventually become immobile. Their oldest ancestors never cared who brought the food and who kept scavengers at bay. This often took several people, resulting in the development of a pack culture wherein the sexual breakdown of the pack is irrelevant to its members.

Terlix young are refereed to as kittens, the terran term entered mass adoption as slang post-first contact after Terlix observed domestic feline kittens and went "Oh yeah, that's what our kids are like.". In the centuries sense it's become the proper term. Their kittens grow very slowly, taking nearly 15 years to reach their full size, with most of that growth occurring between 13 and 15 years of age.

The pack raises children communally with kittens never out of sight by at least two packmemebers. Terlix never think of anyone as their mother or father, and when speaking to other species will use the terms "pack" to mean parents, while referring to individuals within it as "aunt" or "uncle".

Children are expected to form their own packs around the age of 50, and will typically group up with 4-5 others to form their own pack.

NOTE: While they reproduce rarely, Terlix are highly sexual to the point where they have several evolutionary adaptations for non-reproductive sex. The most notable in terms of behavior is a total acceptance of non-straight orientations. Encountering bigotry against gay people confuses a Terlix at best, and enrages them at worst as their species sees sex as a mere activity to engage in with people close to you. One that is capable of creating new life, but to them that's a small side note. The primary purpose is to express affection.

Their physical adaptations are often erroneously believed to be the result of genetic modification. Similar to the spotted hyenas of Old Earth, a female Terlix's cliterois is capable of erecting and reaching a size which permits penetrative sex. This usually only occurs when a female is feeling dominant and confident prior to arousal. Their males, likewise, have a series of physical adaptations which make being penetrated easy, convenient, and pleasurable for both parties.

These adaptations reinforce their psychological norms to the point where Terlix who choose to take a non-Terlix partner are often extremely confused as to the lack of them. They are also often shocked when encountering someone who doesn't want to sleep with them due to their sexual orientation, as most Terlix simply do not care about the sex or gender of their partners.

The (Bioneural) Power of Friendship

The deepest quirk of Terlix psychology is friendship. Terlix make friends rarely. The process is slow, instinctive, and not entirely under conscious control. A Terlix either bonds with someone or they don't, and the process cannot be meaningfully rushed nor can they intentionaly get along with those they dislike without extensive effort on both parties part. But when they do bond, the result exceeds most species' conception of friendship by such a margin that outsiders consistently misread it as romantic.

The distinction becomes clear when you observe a Terlix romantic partnership, which functions as a gestalt entity. They become so thoroughly intertwined in goals, habits, and identity that separating them feels like splitting a person. They are also exceptionally rare, with most Terlix only having (and being perfectly content with) friends.

The reason behind this is an evolutionary adaptation which is their equivalent of adrenaline. A friend's scent, sound, and visual clues cause a Terlix to produce more neurotransmitters, hormones, and ensymes, effectivly overclocking their physical and mental abilities. There is a ceiling to this, but it does mean that while a lone Terlix is sentient and can be quite intelligent, they tend to be somewhat lazy like most predators. Then a friend shows up and they become more active, physically and mentally. The average Terlix continues to be boosted until 18-22 individuals they consider friends are within the range of their senses. This puts them into a range comparable to the 99th percentile of humans.

Notably, this effect works regardless of species. So long as a Trelix conidiers someone (or even a pet) to be a friend, their body reacts as if they were another Terlix. It also works if their friends are not physically present, but they can access a sample of their pheromones. It's common for Terlix solgiers to keep a sealed vile containing samples of their friends hair for emergencies.

Thus a large group of Terlix are able to handle much more than they could alone. Terlix often express confusion when they learn many humans see the power of friendship as a cheezey trope found only within media. Other species are genuinely shocked when Terlix attribute an unlikely victory to the power of friendship. For them, it's not only real, but measurable, demonstrable, and historically how they developed civilization.

Government

Terlix unified in a manner that standard species find difficult to model. There is have formal structure annalists can point at. There is no constitution, no elected body, no mechanism for appointment or removal. What exists is a Council of Elders; the oldest members of their species, who choose to participate in collective decision-making on a volentier basis. Authority accrues naturally to those who have survived long enough and accumulated enough bonds across enough packs that their judgment is trusted by the people who matter. A Terlix who lives three centuries and is still functional has, by definition, been making survivable decisions for three centuries. That is considered sufficient qualification by the overwhelming majority of their species.

The system works because Terlix psychology makes it work. An elder who begins making bad decisions loses the bonds that give them influence, and bond loss is more motivating to a Terlix than any legal consequence. Humans find this baffling. There is nothing to audit, nothing to appeal, no document that grants or removes power. Terrans understand the gist of the system, and can respect it works for them.

Externally, Terlix find it difficult to deal with other species nations due to how often they tend to change leadership, and how little life experience leaders usually have. Their greatest ally is the Terran Protectorate simply because it is ultimately led by a single AI system that's been in charge for thousands of years. That is something they can respect and intuitively understand. To the point where that AI (Central) was invited to the Council of Elders to aid in space related decision making.

Religion

The Terlix have no dominant faith. Packs and meta-packs (their term for towns) developed their own spiritual traditions over millennia, and the resulting diversity is vast enough that summarizing it is not really possible. What little can be said is that Terlix respect philosophical religions such as Buddism far more than those which make claims about reality. The phrase curiosity killed the cat applies very much tot he Terlix. If a claim is made, they want proof of it. If it cannot be proven, it is seen as a lie and lies are insults.
