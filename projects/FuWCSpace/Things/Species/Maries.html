Maries

Maries (a portmanteau of Martian and fairy), properly called Eglwyswrw (Good luck. Just use the nickname), are diminutive, bioluminescent humanoids with features resembling terrestrial insects and fey-like creatures. Standing at an average height of 27-31 cm (~10-12 inches), their dragonfly-esq wings and vibrant coloration serve as camouflage adaptations to the nearly-extinct Martian jungles. Both sexes exhibit strikingly translucent skin in tones of green, yellow, and blue, with males typically displaying brighter and more iridescent patterns. Their haircolor is determined by the flora their mother consumes during pregnancy, and typically comes out as a blue or pink due to their preferred diets.

Their anatomy incorporates fine, chitinous exoskeletal layers beneath the skin, providing lightweight protection while allowing for remarkable flexibility. Despite their fragile appearance, they are surprisingly resilient, though their muscular systems adapted to their low-gravity Martian environment. As they evolved in a 0.38 G environment, they are not capable of long-term survival in environments humans prefer without significant genetic modifications and cybernetic implants (Such as a thrust-pack so they can still fly in what is nearly 3x their homeworld's gravity).

Another problem with higher gravity environments is also related to their wings, but has nothing to do with flight. Maries use low-frequency vibrations used for echolocation and communication, which they generate via their wings. They can also speak, but their voices do not carry far.

Maries make use of Bioluminescent Communication in addition to auditory communication. They possess specialized chromatophores with enough resolution, brightness, and color varience to optically communicate complex emotions and ideas. This visual language is particularly vital in the darker corners of their subterranean world.

Their other primary cave based adaptation is an extremely efficient digestive system. Maries produce almost no waste, despite sleeping only 45 minutes in a 12 hour span and remaining highly active. What little waste they expel is extremely dense and rich with minerals, albeit ones their bodies cannot metabolize. Various fungi evolved to take advantage of this system of infrequent but dense "food" supplies. This forms the core of Marie agriculture.

Awelion

Maries inhabit a vast underground cave system found beneath Gusev Crater called Awelion. It is a fascinating place visited by few aside from the Maries themselves. This is entirely an issue of scale, as while many chambers could comfortably fit many human sized individuals, most humans cannot navigate between them simply due to their size. The cave system is truly labyrinthine, with frequent use of small passages needed to get anywhere within its microcosm of ancient Mars.

Awelion is famed for its bioluminescent gardens, communal nesting chambers, luminescent fungi, towering phosphoric crystal formations, and a unique biosphere reliant on geothermal energy and ancient aquifers. It is insulated from the harsh surface conditions by layers of hard stone, accessible from a single cave opening located ironically just behind the crest of the hill seen within Spirit's final image. Had it progressed another 14 meters it would have fallen into a small clearing the Maries refer to as the "Skyhole Basin" with its camera pointing at the tourist center.

Cultural Nuances

Maries have developed a highly communal society emphasizing harmony and equilibrium with their environment. This is not out of any particular love of nature, but rather due to living in what amounts to a biodome. Anything they take must be replenished as there is no outside world to use for additional resources. This has made the Maries some of the most impressive recyclers, upcyclers, and maintainers in the galaxy.

According to the log of the TPS Wild Ride, a Terran freighter, a Marie passenger was able to stop a reactor meltdown using some chewing gum, steel wire, and a bit of ductape. She had no idea this was a human idiom for extreme ingenuity, rather she simply understood the material properties of the items and knew what needed to be done to repair the coolant line... and had no idea spare coolant lines were abord and standard protocol was to replace a damaged line.

Marie societal structure revolves around Songweaving, a complex practice blending oral tradition, storytelling, and bioluminescent signaling. These rituals form the backbone of their cultural identity, used to convey history, warn of danger, and even establish intricate mating dances. The primary purpose however is to create a sense of "greater self" through the memorization of their people's history as it pertains to one's own life.

For example while a carpenter would know the songs relating to great carpenters within their history as well as some of the most amazing works of creation within their field, a baker would not, but instead know the songs related to baking and baked goods. Marie music is composed such that it sounds not only incomplete, but actually bad if not sung in harmony with the other songs. As a result, only when a Marie community sings together the "magic" happens and their songs become good. Wonderous even. And the Marie are some of the biggest music lovers in known space.

This feat of social engineering to promote harmony has been literally applauded by Central.

The Marie believe their ancestors were once caretakers of Mars's surface, tasked with maintaining ecological balance. The devastation of Mars's surface, referred to in their myths as "The Great Quieting," is a source of sorrow and cautionary tales. They view themselves as custodians of the last untouched vestiges of Mars's natural beauty and treat their habitat with almost religious reverence.

At least, nearly all of them do. A large number of them enjoy all things mechanical and technological and typically leave home for other worlds simply to be able to live in their preferred "biome". Their kin hold no ill will against them for this, and see it as good that their population occasionally shrinks so as to remain stable.

Interactions with Humans

Humans made their first unaided space flight in a human designed vessel on the 8th of September, 2017. The mission was the brainchild and sole work of private citizen Kate Anschutz, granddaughter of the business mogul Philip Anschutz. The soul purpose of the mission was to prevent Elon Musk from being the first man on Mars in a purely spiteful move relating to calling Kate's concept orbital return pod "stupid" on X.

Kate picked Gusev Crater as her landing target, deciding that she'd stream the entire trip and pick up Spirit as proof she'd been to Mars. She landed near the entrance to Awelion and discovered a small group of Marie engineers building a copy of Spirit next to the orgional roverLater claiming to be doing so because "she looked lonly. Everyone needs a friend."leading to their first contact, and humans second contact.

The Marie have been left alone, as per their wishes. They are not part of the Terran Protectorate, but as they live in the Sol System, they are protected by Terran might. For the most part, they simply wish to be left alone and keep the last Jungle of Mars alive. Concequently humans have left Mars unterraformed, and leave the entirety of Gusev Crater alone regardless of operations on Mars. Presently the Marie are requesting human aid in expanding the Jungle into the crater as a whole. The Protectorate seems amicable to their request and is likely to comply.

This is because some human scientists see the Maries as a living link to Mars's ancient history, a treasure trove of evolutionary biology and planetary science within the Sol System. Any deal on this front would involve premission to study flora and fauna from Awelion.

Other Maries, a minority, have been granted Terran citizenship as individuals at their request. This is due to their species seemingly having two biocastes, one of whom is fulfilled by botanical endeavors, the other by mechanical interests. These Engineer Fairies (as many call them) often leave Awelion behind to seek a life where they can tinker, toil, and build with metal and lightning rather than earth and wood. These Maries can be found on many Terran ships and star cities, though are somewhat territorial and have a population density dependent on how much stuff their is to fix / build in a given area.

Societal Tensions

The Martian Maries maintain a wary distance from human settlements and mining operations, but not all factions within their society agree on how to approach humanity. The Wings of Light, a faction advocating for cautious diplomacy, believe that humans might be allies in revitalizing Marss ecosystems. Presently they are winning their nation's culture war. Conversely, the Deepkeepers, a more traditionalist group, view humans as invaders to guard their realm from at all costs. Presently they are loosing. Primarily because Maries recently learned of an ancient human weapon, the atom bomb. They know they have no way to protect their realm against one.

They have no idea that humans would never attack them.

Mystical Perception

Although their abilities are entirely explainable through biology, humans often describe Maries as magical. Their bioluminescent songs, intricate gardens of glowing fungi, and gravity-defying flight evoke fairy tale imagery. Potentially due to an expedition the Maries sent to Earth in the early 300s CE, though the expedition was lost with no records having emerged. This perception leads to conflicting attitudessome humans romanticize and idolize them, while others regard them as mere curiosities.
