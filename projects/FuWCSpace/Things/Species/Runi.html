Species Profile

Basic Information

  • Name: Runi
  • Scientific Classification/Taxonomy: (if applicable)
  • Common Names: Furfolk, animals (derogatory), TeSiHL (Pron. Tea-sul): Terrifying Simulacra of Humanoid Lifeforms (Derogatory or Complementary depending on user.)
  • Origin/Primary Habitat: The Planet Runa
  • Population Distribution: Most landmasses and seas on Runa
  • Hyper Polymorphic: Ruini are polymorphic, with over 300,000 known morphs. A morph is a unique morphological appearance derived from an Earth animal species, filtered through a general humanoid appearance. All Runi are the same internally and have internal anatomy consistent with Runaic species.
  • Mutants: Runi are artificially induced mutants created by a teraforming accident which induced DNA from Earth's species into a now-extinct Runaic ancestor species, the vitrivom.

The Runi are a species of mutants resulting from a terraforming accident which infused a Runa-endemic species with DNA from Earth. At the surface level Runi appear to be a multitude of different species, and can be easily mistaken for one of humanity's many anthropomorphic or "furry" clans. In fact the Runi are one species which has the highest number of morphs of any polymorphic species known to the Protectorate.

The Runi's resemblance to Earth life comes from their genetic sequence being roughly 45% earth-based (primarily human), with the rest coming from the vitrivorm, (an extinct Runaic species). Their human DNA primarily influences their body shape and has granted sapience to their species. Their earth-animal DNA determines their morph and can grant some related abilities such as flight, water breathing, enhanced endurance, and other such animal abilities.

Their alien DNA covers everything else. Under their morph a Runi's biology is extremely similar to the rest of Runa's life forms, including the biological implantation of crystals. Biocrystals are a near-universal evolutionary adaptation of life on Runa. They have an extreme variance of purpose within the planet's life forms depending on their evolutionary history. In the Niandr's case, the bio gems store mineral-based toxins in a mater similar to the human appendix.

The Runi's gems serve several purposes, as they inherited a variety of crystals from their vitrivorm ancestors. Their skeletal system uses a crystalline structure to produce a hard-yet-elastic cartilage system which features armor plates for their heart. Their eyes are photosensitive crystals encased in a bio-capsule which link to their brains using flexible carbon based glass" fibers" making their optic system optical rather than electrical. The majority of brain structures are crystalline in nature, making their brains" organic photonic computers.

Exotic certainty, but normal for Runaic life due to the planet's abundance of organic based crystal" flora".

Runi blood is almost identical to their ancestor species. Its key proteins glow via phosphorescence and if electrified at low voltages. Rani blood glows a bright blue-green if exposed to daylight. This glow can last for between 2 to 2.5 hours. In their bodies, their blood will basic form with climate. As an example, a Runi in a cold environment will bulk up over several days, not with muscle but with a fat-like substance that's purely an insulator. This adaptation will reverse if they return to a warmer climate.

Other notable vitrivormic inheritances include:

  • Runi's organs are either perfectly semetrical and centered, or have a mirrored twin on the other side of their body. Even their intestinal tract makes use of "duplicate" intestines to keep this symmetry, a trait found only in animals descended from the vitrivorm's ancestorial species.
    • This quirk has made their medical science uniquely complex and distinct from other humanoids.
  • A Runi's upper and lower canine teeth extend when theyre hungry or stressed, a remnant of the Vitrivorms predator instincts. This trait is considered embarrassing in polite society but is also seen as a mark of raw honesty or vulnerability.
    • Their teeth are far superior at cutting flesh and tough plant material when extended.
  • Runi fertility is tied to food abundance. Their mating instincts remain constant regardless of food availability, but a certain caloric intake threshold must be met for a period of several months for a Runi to begin producing gametes.
    • Runi who do not wish to have children at present can simply skip lunch every few days.
  • Runi genetics are unusually malleable, retaining traces of their origin. This adaptability allows for greater physical and cognitive diversity due to a higher mutation chance than most other lifeforms, but of course this is a double edged sword that also elevates the chance of negative traits emerging in a gene pool.

The most intrasting and power full of the Rani's Xeno form traits is their psionic powers. They are all evolutions of their ancestors, though rather than working at range they require physical contact. The typical powers are as follows:

  1. Tactile Empathy: Rani share emotions through physical contact. This is not an optional or controllable effect. If they make contact, both Runi will experience the other's current emotions and any particularly strongly remembered emotions they are consciously thinking of at the time. This ability only works between Runi, and makes acts like hugging and handshakes very culturally significant. Handholding is so revered and taboo due to this it is only preformed between best friends (which are the Runi equiviant to a partner / mate). Touch is naturally avoided with strangers as much as possible.
    1. Note: Many Runi like Terran species and will attempt to befriend them. As friendship is the first step to a romantic relationship for Runi, Terrans with a Runi friend are urged to get a biosculpt to share this tactile ability with members of the Runi species as quickly as possible as it is critical to the mantance of friendships.
  2. Dream Sharing: Runi have very vivid dreams, which can be shared with anyone they are in physical contact with while sleeping. This can be a blessing or a curse instance to instance as Runi can either share a unique experience, or relive a trauma with company.
  3. Crystalic Resonance Reading: Rani can feel the energy patterns within their homeworld's crystals. This energy is usual y electrical, but on occasion is psionic. The Rani believe certain specific crystals" sing" with the dreams of their ancestors. This forms the core of the majority of their religions.

A much more rare Rani power is found in -1% of their population. Their psionic powers augment their body's adaptability leading to physical adaptation to the existence of psionic power. The end result being the manifestation of what can only be called super powers. A Powered Runi typically has one minor ability, such as the ability to bond small objects together as if by glue. Roughly 5% of Powered Runi have powers sufficient for practical application to a trade, and 10% can apply their powers to be the traditional concept of a superhero or villein.

Runi societies expect roughly a 1:700 ratio of Powered Runi (IE: Runi with super powers). Consequently all Runi cultures have methods for supporting people with such abilities, and for handling bad actors with them as well. These methods vary, from culture to culture and are generally found in most if not all of their settlements.

Their societies have some other universal features based on their biology. Their resonance sense makes crystals quite important; they are often used for totems tablets and cultural touchstone objects. " Linking" a process where a criminal is forced to hold hand with their victim to relive the victim experiences are common. Dream Guides, trained professionals who enter dreams with people, are a form of therapist universal to Runi cultures.

The most interesting universal cultural touchstone for Runi based on their biology is related to their reproductive process and Tactile Empathy. Runi are demisexual, in other words they can only feel sexual attraction to someone only after they've developed a close emotional bond with them. They do not care about the sex or gender of their partners at all, they only care about someone they feel emotionally close to, a state they call "resonance".

As a result, Runi are typically sexually active with most of their friend group, and should they have a best friend that relationship is analogous to a spouse or mate, save the relationship is always open. Runi do not have a taboo for sex like most Terran species do. Instead, their taboo is for reproduction itself.

A Runi who wishes to have children seeks a "partner", allows themselves to become fertile (they can sense when they are) and will mate with their partner exactly enough times to become pregnant, and then no more as they usually do not feel attracted to one another (Partners are chosen for genetic compatibility and likelihood of healthy offspring, not romance, love, lust, or any other factor). The female will then carry her clutch until it is time for them to be laid and incubated, at which time the other partner is informed and arrangements for raising the children post-hatching are solidified.

Runi raise children in a manner similar to divorced human parents, transferring children between the care of their father and their best friend, and then their mother and their best friend typically on a weekly or daily basis. This process is not harmful to a developing Runi's mind or spirit and indeed seems integral to allowing them to grow into a well adjusted adult.

Their name for themselves translates roughly as the people of Runa, while Runa itself is a word with many meanings depending on the context in which it is used. The most broad meaning is best translated as the natural world including man and all man made things, ie a functional equivalent to universe. The contextual part of the word Runa is the scale of the space it is being used to refer to. It can mean anything from a single room, to a town, a nation, a continent, a planet, or in fact any location which has both a given volume and has a natural harmony to it. Runa is an adverb, not a proper noun however, with the sole exception of when referring to the Runis home planet as a whole as they long believed there was nothing beyond it.
