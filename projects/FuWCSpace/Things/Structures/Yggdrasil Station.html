Yggdrasil Station

Basic Information

  • Name: Yggdrasil Station
    • Other Names: Yggdrasil Star City, Yggdrasil
  • Type: Mobile Star City
  • Population: 2,934,527
  • Size: 810 km
  • Primary Language(s): Terran
  • Notable Species: Humans (Sciurid Clan)
  • Location: Presently docked with the Sol Dyson Sphere

Description

Yggdrasil Station is a mobile Terran Star City which serves as the home of the Sciurid Clan, one of the many Human Clans. The station is classified as a station despite being mobile as its engines are not designed to be operated for more than a few days at a time and its FTL is limited to the use of |Hyperlanes and being towed by jumpships. Its maximum speed of 0.13C precludes it from classification as a starship, though its residents argue it should be classed as one. Especialy given its purpose.

Yggdrasil's primary mission is simple: to seek out new science, to build new outposts, to bring people together on the final frontier.

Yggdrasil is capable of landing on most planetary bodies (Including aquatic landings) and is frequently located in deep space. While slow to move itself, it carries a large complement of vessels, enough to support three separate fleets. Presently these fleets are:

  1. The 98th Logistics Wing
    1. 148 vessels, mostly freighters, some escort fighters, 3 frigates.
  2. Lagrivea (A mercenary group specializing in defense.)
    1. 212 vessels, mostly interceptors, fighter-bombers, 10 corvettes, 1 frigate
  3. 3rd Interstellar Explorer's Corps
    1. 14 Jacob-Class science vessels, 4 Gray-class engineering vessels

The entire station is dedicated to its mission, and over its 800 years of service have been some of the most storied explorers in the Protectorate's history. They were the first to reach the Galactic Core, and the first to reach a Satalight Galaxy (not counting the Jumpship which gave them a tow across the intersteller gulf).

a long-term diplomatic station for the Terran Protectorate. It technically mobile, but usually remains in place within a star system for decades, occasionally centuries. Its mission is simple, present an honest view of Terran civilization to newly discovered life in the hopes of fostering positive long-term relationships.

Currently Reshi is assigned to the planet Runa in the HV 2112 system. Its posting followed the answer of an age old mystery relating to the Gaia Ascendant and its disappearance. Reshi is currently primarily handling the creation of system infrastructure for the inhabitants of Runa as compensation for the unintentional terraforming resulting from the the Ascendant's wreck. The idea being once both of the planet's sapient species are able to reach the stars the infra will be turned over to them. Hopefuly by which time they will be on friendly terms with the Protectorate.

Reshi is a no-compromise look at Terran civilization. Its design, arcature, features, citizenry, industry, and every other system are all designed to be as quintessentially Terran as possible, warts and all. Its various sectors provide honest looks at the most common types of people and their cultures, faults and all.

Physicaly Reshi Station is a Stanford Torus style megastructurea doughnut-shaped ring 24 kilometers in diameter and rotating at 0.27 RPM to simulate Earth-standard gravity. Reshi's interior contains sculpted terrain and synthetic ecospheres along with a holographic sky. Aside from the horizon curving upwards at the "north" and "south" one could be mistake the station's interior for a mountain valley. Its total inhabitable area covers slightly more than 450 square kilometers, housing nearly 900,000 permanent residents and serves as a fully self sufficient settlement.

Key features include:

  • Artificial gravity generated by rotation, with emergency grav-plating.
  • A self-sustaining ecosystem, featuring synthetic weather systems and cultivated environments.
  • Zero-gravity industrial hubs in its non-rotating central axis for manufacturing and research.
  • Mass transit systems connecting districts via the hub and spokes.

Districts

Reshi is split into to nine cultural districts, a practice common on all large Terran settlements. Each district is semi-autonomous in terms of civil law, allowing for cultural rules and norms designed to benefit and enable the citizens who live within to have the lives they most desire. A list of Reshi's districts follows:

  1. Ketis District:
    A lifestyle-focused district centered on kink and fetish communities, exemplifying open-mindedness and personal freedom. The presence of Blue Hills gives this district significant diplomatic importance.
  2. Aurora District:
    A cultural and artistic hub. This district celebrates creativity in all its forms, from traditional art to digital media, music, dance, and experimental works. The district is filled with galleries, theaters, and open-air performance spaces, as well as vibrant street art. It also houses the Terran Galactic Cultural Exchange Center, showcasing humanity's artistic heritage to alien visitors.
  3. Osiris District:
    A green district dedicated to agriculture, environmental science, and sustainability. This district boasts towering hydroponic farms, synthetically engineered forests, and parks teeming with plant and animal life from Earth and beyond. It's both a food production center and a testament to Terran ecological stewardship, showcasing how humans have learned to harmonize with nature even in artificial environments.
  4. Aegis District:
    The stations security and defense hub, housing PSCPS headquarters and the local branch of the Terran Protectorate Navy. Its a district where Terran values of discipline, service, and cooperation shine, showcasing state-of-the-art technology and military precision. It also includes public-facing institutions like a "Peacekeepers' Museum" to highlight humanity's efforts at fostering interstellar peace.
  5. Eudaimonia District:
    A philosophical and educational enclave, home to universities, think tanks, and research institutions. This district embodies the Terran pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, featuring libraries with data spanning human history and alien cultures, along with forums for debate and public lectures. Its motto: A better tomorrow, built today.
  6. Aether District:
    Focused on cutting-edge technology, innovation, and cyberculture. This district is filled with AI research labs, robotics workshops, and tech start-ups, often blurring the line between humans and machines. Neon-lit streets host VR cafes and augmented reality parks, providing a glimpse of Terran technological mastery.
  7. Mosaic District:
    A melting pot district that celebrates multiculturalism, representing the diverse ethnic and cultural heritage of Earth. Each neighborhood reflects the aesthetics and traditions of different human cultures, reimagined in futuristic ways. From bustling markets to fusion cuisine restaurants, this district demonstrates how Terrans have preserved and evolved their roots in a galactic context.
  8. Horizon District:
    A "slice of home" district identical to the living conditions of most urban-dwelling Terrans. It provides a comfortable and familiar space for Terrans and serves as a cultural touchstone for alien visitors to understand traditional human life. Reshi's unique spin on the classic Terran housing concept is biosculpted flora and fauna that mimics the species found on whatever world the station is currently parked over.
  9. Solace District:
    A wellness-focused district emphasizing healthcare, mental health, and recreation. It includes advanced medical facilities, spas, mindfulness centers, and synthetic natural environments where residents can de-stress. This district reflects the Terran commitment to quality of life and self-care, making it a frequent destination for diplomats and alien visitors alike.
