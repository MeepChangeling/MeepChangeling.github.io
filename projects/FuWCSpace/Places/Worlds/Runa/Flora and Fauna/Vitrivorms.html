Vitrivorms

  • Name: Vitrivorms (Latin: "glass worms")
    • Name is of human origin.
    • Original name lost to Runa's apocalypse.
    • Driven extinct by the apocalypse, hence no name.
  • Extinction Status: Extinct, formerly endemic to Runa.
  • Classification: Pseudoinvertebrate/gelatinous multicellular organism.
    • Possibly sapient. Experiments ongoing and inconclusive.
  • Size: ~11.5 meters in length and 0.5 meters in diameter when fully grown and at rest. Highly morphable, capable of compressing their body into small crevices, similar to an octopus.
  • Body Structure: Comprised of a semi-gelatinous, translucent outer layer with embedded crystalline structures that functioned both as sensory organs and for limited bioluminescent communication. Beneath this outer layer were dense, flexible muscle-like filaments arranged symmetrically around a central nutrient-processing core. The outer layer could be tightened to create a rigid structure similar to Runa's surface crystals, allowing them to hide amongst crystal patches.
  • Mobility: Moved through a combination of undulating motion similar to snakes and worms. Could also extend small pseudopods that allowed them to grab objects and pull themselves along surfaces.
  • Diet: Omnivorous, feeding on tough plant matter and small animals. Photovoltaic, required extended periods within the sunlight, specifically with their biocrystals exposed to the light to charge electrolytic cells. These cells provide energy for many of the vitrivorm's biological process but most notably the synthesis of several vitamin compounds no longer found in Runa's flora.
  • Habitat: Predominantly forest dwellers, thriving in Runas sprawling temperate forests. Their translucent appearance helped them camouflage among the crystalline deposits and woody vegetation. Always found near cave entrances, caves would always contain mineral rich pools of water (Fresh or saliene).

Key Biological Traits:

  1. Biogems: Like most of the planet's lifeforms, vitrivorms incorporate mineral deposits into their biology which form gemstone-like structures that protrude from their skin. In their case, these organs serve as sensory inputs, photovoltaic cells, and psionic antennas.
  2. Metamorphic Tissue: Their tissues were highly adaptable and could reorganize themselves into different forms to suit environmental changes in real time. They were able to adapt to hot, cold, wet, dry, and even irradiated conditions within minutes. This adaptability allowed them to occupy a variety of ecological niches, especially those at the boundaries of different biomes.
  3. Symmetry and Armor: Vitrivorms had a hardened central cartilage-like spine and plates to protect their nutrient core. Other vital areas were protected by thinner plates to ensure flexibility. While resistant to punctures and cutting, their cartilage structure was extremely elastic and had little to no effect on mobility.
  4. Environmental Dependent Reproduction: Vitrivorms were hermaphroditic but required environmental triggers such as specific chemical signals in their diet to produce viable gametes. Notably this did not impact their reproductive urges. Vitrivorms would attempt reproduction with packmates seemingly for fun, regardless of their current fertility.
  5. Crystalline Sensory Structures: Embedded crystals in their gelatinous body served as multifunctional sensory organs, akin to compound eyes or lateral lines in fish. These crystals could:
    • Detect light across multiple wavelengths, including ultraviolet and infrared.
    • Sense vibrations in the air or ground.
    • Emit faint bioluminescence in intricate patterns for communication, mating displays, and as a deterrent to predators.
  6. Pseudopod Adaptations: Their retractable pseudopods werent just for movement but also for manipulating objects and capturing prey. Each pseudopod had fine, hair-like cilia that allowed them to taste surfaces, sense chemicals, and extract nutrients directly.
  7. Self-Healing Membranes: Their semi-liquid outer layer could quickly heal from minor injuries by redistributing proteins and cellular materials.
  8. Internal Organs: Beneath the nutrient core, vitrivorms possessed two auxiliary cores: one for metabolic regulation and another for psionic signal amplification.

Behavior and Social Structure

  1. Social Intelligence: Though not eusocial, Vitrivorms demonstrated complex group behavior. They communicated using bioluminescent pulses and pheromonal signals, forming loose, cooperative groups to share resources and protect one another.
    • In areas of high population density, their bioluminescent signals formed patterns that resembled early language or code.
  2. Optical Camouflage: By altering the refractive index of their gelatinous body, they could appear nearly invisible in dense forest environments.
    • This process was very energy intensive, and is only rarely used by reproduced vitrivorms. Typically only when they believe themselves to be in mortal danger.
      • Reshaping to appear as a pile of crystals appears to be their favored means of stealth.
    • In defense, they could emit a sudden, bright flash of light from their crystals to startle predators and flee.
  3. Reproduction (Expanded): Reproduction involved a mix of external and internal fertilization, depending on environmental triggers. During the mating season, vitrivorms gathered in mineral-rich pools, where their gametes combined in nutrient-laden water, accelerating embryonic development. These pools were occasionally guarded by older vitrivorms during the rest of the year as other species would make use of the pools.
    • Juveniles were entirely transparent until their crystalline structures began to develop.

Ecological Nish

Vitrivorms were critical to Ancient Runa's ecosystems. By processing tough vegetation and smaller organisms, they helped maintain a balance between plant overgrowth and herbivore populations. Their waste products enriched the soil, fostering plant growth. This symbiotic relationship with Runas ecosystem had to be synthetically recreated by the Niandr to restore their ability to farm in their traditional breadbaskets.

Psionic Abilities

Vitrivorms crystalline sensory organs werent limited to physical senses. These structures allowed them to project and receive psionic energy, communicating over short distances without physical interaction. It is unknown if this communication was complex. Sapience assessments are ongoing but suggest that vitriforms are semi-sapient.

Their telepathy was not their only psionic power. They express mild tactile telekinesis. While it is not stronger than they are on their own it allows them to grip any object they touch with great force. The force required to dislodge a vitrivorms' grip exceeds the tensile strength of their flesh.

Extinction:

Vitrivorms were driven to extinction when ancient Niandr scientists unintentionally activated a half-disassembled ancient Terran terraforming vessel which had made planetfall on their world before shutting down. The activation resulted in the bioseeding process being unable to find the vessel's stored clone slugs, resulting in a fallback protocol searching for them, locating nearby populations of vitrivorms, and after determining them to be a 98% match to the clone slugs, used drones to use them as the base for the scrambled DNA profiles incarnation.

Any vitriforms not used in this process faced starvation. Vitrivorms, dependent on sunlight for crystal growth and bioluminescent communication were likely unable to handle the planet's dim years when combined with the extra darkness due to terraforming gasses floating within the atmosphere. It is possible isolated groups survived near the equator, but none have been found.

The end result is the birth of the Runi species at the expense of vitrivorms. No wild populations of vitrivorms have been found in 20 years of searching. The species has been recreated through genetic examination of the Runi species producing enough fragments of their genome to recreate them via cloning. Currently vitrivorms can be found in select zoos and as pets. There is a plan to reintroduce them into select habitats on Runa to combat invasive plant species.

Influence on the Runi

The Runi inherited the following from their vitrivorm genetics.

  • The Vitrivorms metamorphic tissue heavily influenced the genetic polymorphism seen in the Runi, enabling their extreme phenotypic diversity.
  • Their crystalline sensory structures may explain latent psionic traits or sensitivities in the Runi, such as vulnerability to human tactile psionics.
  • The central armor plating protecting the nutrient core likely inspired the Runis symmetrical and hardened internal organ placements.
